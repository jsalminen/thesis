A Socially Developed Product™

.

  • Make Sure Your Idea is Feasible

    Quirky focuses on physical consumer products, so no business ideas, food, or standalone software please. We like app-enabled hardware ideas, through. Always be sure to ask yourself: will my product make people’s lives better, how will it work, and will people buy it? These are some of the questions we’ll be asking.

    .

  • Read the Blog

    Make sure to read our blog at www.quirky.com/blog. There you’ll find everything Quirky-related, including Quirky University, our blog resource full of design tidbits and inside tips on how to craft your Quirky submission.

    .

  • Perfect the Art of the 140-Character Pitch

    The 140-character product description is your big chance to bring people to your idea page. Use it wisely! Be as clear and specific as possible, and try to avoid SHOUTING AT PEOPLE IN ALL CAPS. Correct grammar doesn’t hurt either.

    .

  • Clearly Describe the Problem and Solution or Product and Customer

    We believe that some of the best products come out of universal problems, so describe yours and the solution you’ve come up with clearly and concisely.

    .

  • Do Your Research

    Do a thorough market and patent search, and include a list of similar products with your idea submission. It’s better that you bring up potential competitors and argue for what makes your idea different, than have someone else do it for you.

    .

  • Attach, Attach, Attach

    Our community is made up of visual thinkers. The more you illustrate, the more excited they're bound to get. So upload images, embed YouTube videos... go nuts. A few things to keep in mind: images will only appear as preview thumbnails if they're under 10MB in JPG, PNG, or GIF formats. Videos can be added by cutting-and-pasting the video embed code, which is available on most hosting sites via the “Share” button.

    .

  • Engage with the Community

    The best ideators check their comments section often and provide friendly replies, responses, and rebuttals. Don’t be surprised if you have some critics: consider any criticism and use it to perfect your idea.

    .

  • Approve or Reject Suggestions

    Your peers can help refine your submission and suggest additional features or improvements to make it really stand out. Check your revision suggestions often by clicking “Suggestions” at the top of your ideation page.

    .

  • Use Your Real Name

    We give you the option to use an alias, but we prefer real names to fake ones. If your idea is chosen we’ll want to know who is the genius behind it.

    .

  • Specify Your Skills

    Do you have mad graphic design skills? Are you a gourmet chef, or a fitness guru? Tagging your specific specialties in your member profile shows off your expertise and interests. Now’s not the time to be humble; show off your skills!

    .

  • Give Props Where Props Are Due

    When a community member does good work, give ‘em a pat on the back. Just head on over to that user's profile page and give him/her a prop, or public message of praise.

    .

  • Follow and be Followed

    Following makes it easy to keep up with other inventors and to see when they submit new ideas. Want to know how successful community members do it? Check out the activity stream on their profile page to get an idea for how they participate.

    .

  • Message Other Members

    Messaging provides a private way for you to communicate directly with other members. Simply click "Send Message" on someone’s profile page to write and send one. Keep in mind that members need to be following you before you can send them messages.

    .

  • Converse in the Forum

    The community forums provide spaces for public communication about new features, products, sales techniques, and general water cooler discussions. For support questions visit our support forums. Remember to be civil and open minded!

    .

  • Share the Quirky Love With Social Sales

    Invitations can be found on the “Social Sales” page of your Account Dashboard. Invite friends and family via email, and you can earn $5 for their first idea and $5 if they buy a product. You can also share the referral link found on each product page to earn $5 on sales to new customers.

    .

  • Use Twitter, Facebook, Pinterest, Etc.

    Use social media to generate interest in Quirky. Re-Tweet product launches using your social sales link, pin images of products to your Pinterest boards, and remind your followers and friends about ongoing projects. The more people that click on your social sales link, the more cash you're likely to earn.

    .

  • Pick a Product

    The best way to drive sales is to focus. Pick a specific product you love and promote it like crazy using your social sales link. Introduce the product to relevant communities and reach out to influential bloggers in that category. Persistence pays off.

    .

  • Think Outside the Box

    Design social sales-linked banners for your blog or Facebook page. Create a micro-site for a specific product. Be creative in finding ways to drive traffic to Quirky, because Quirky sales and new submissions = money in your pocket.

    .

  • Participate In Everything

    You can earn influence for the smallest actions or the largest contributions: from voting or revising idea submissions, to submitting a designs and names for a products in development. You can even help us set the price of products before they hit store shelves!

    .

  • Stay Active

    Things move quickly here at Quirky, and projects open daily. Check back frequently if you want to make a real impact on the products in development. If you want an easy way to stay abreast of new projects open for participation, follow us on Twitter.

    .

  • Cast Your Votes Wisely

    We’re counting on you to bubble up the best ideas in each round. Do the process justice by voting for ideas you really believe in! You get 15 votes a day for invention submissions and 3 per project for products in development, so use them wisely.

    .

  • Sort by Category

    The sorting tools on the top of each project page help you quickly find what you are looking for, and easily keep track of ideas. Sort ideas by category, and filter them by activity or what you already participated in.
