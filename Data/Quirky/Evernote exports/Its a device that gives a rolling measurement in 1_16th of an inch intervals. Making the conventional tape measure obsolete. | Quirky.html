Its a device that gives a rolling measurement in 1/16th of an inch intervals. Making the conventional tape measure obsolete.

Electronics
Submitted by: Terry Tallman
.

The Problem

Saving time and getting as accurate a measurement as you would get when usig a conventional tape measure.Also saving you the aggravation with a conventional tape measure coming loose or flopping around and bending.Or saveing you from slicing your fingers open when they reel back in to it's casing.Also it will make it easier for someone not falmilure with a tape measure to take a measurement something.

The Solution

It save's time by not having to roll out the tape and hooking it to the end of a board or what ever your measuring.Tape's have ends that with use will become inaccurate.It dosen't have moving parts to come loose or flop around or cut you.With a large rolling readout it will make it easy to read and simple to use.

Key Features

  • Unlike a laser measuring tool it gives you a rolling measurement like the readout on digital scales.
  • The device is simple to use with only one button to press.

    Most conventional tape measures can be stiffly extended over of distance of maybe 6 feet or so which is often very handy. I'm not sure how this product would address those situations.

    Also, marking and measuring a board, say cutting 6'8" off an 8' piece of shelving, is much easier with a conventional tape measure. You stand at one end, extend it, hook the far end, and then make your mark. That sounds easier than having to roll this product down the board for every cut.

    I believe the claim of the accuracy of the 'hook' degrading with time is inaccurate. I've used them heavily for years and even if there were a 1/32" or 1/16" (not that I've seen either) amount of wear that would not be significant for most measurements over a foot long.

    One more thing; When measuring the dimensions of a room or area (a very common use) you can use a tape measure while standing up - just run it along the floor and bend it up at the wall. Unless your device has a handle you're going to have to get down on your hands and knees to take the measurement.

    I will grant there are times when a conventional tape can be at a disadvantage; i.e. measuring along a long wall (especially if you don't have someone to hold the far end.) So, there probably are situations where a rolling tape measure is a good idea, and if they don't already have a product targetted for those (i.e. the driveway measuring tool) then perhaps this device can find a niche. But for general purpose measurements a conventional tape measure is a simple, well-thought-out and battery-less device and hard to beat.

    Rolling tape measures already exist in different sizes from hand held to floor types. These will never make standard tape measures obsolete. Although a rolling tape measure may have its place for some things it cannot compare to a standard tape measure for speed and accuracy. As a Quality Technician in a manufacturing facility, I and my coworkers use tape measures throughout the day on a regular basis. I purchased a couple of rolling tape measures to try out of curiosity and found them too slow and inaccurate for what we needed to do. Aligning the wheel and rolling it straight can be challenging, especially on a slippery surface. Measuring inside surfaces in out of the question. I wish you luck with this idea but it will never fill more than a niche market in my opinion.
