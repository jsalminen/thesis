Project Brief

Community Design: Community Design

In this project we want you to submit sketches, images, videos, and other visuals that illustrate design directions for Katherine Buchholz’s Nursing Diaper Bag. We will take the top ideas as inspiration towards the final design.


Designer Tip: Before submitting, please keep in mind that this is a two-part product; a nursing blanket integrated with a diaper bag. We’d love it if you submitted ideas that included these two essential elements.

I was an active Mom with baby. I like to be hands free. I used the duck hunter's vest in this photo to carry diapers, wipes, phone, camera, keys, lipstick, even a bottle. Later it was drink boxes, sunscreen, bubble soap, sidewalk chalk, and pockets to carry back pretty rocks :-) I still use it today for multiple things that I want organized, & the weight dispersed about my body. I think it's a good starting point to add other features. This one is 100% cotton. Very sturdy, but very lightweight. I wore it in South Carolina summers. Not uncomfortable at all. To be used as a privacy garment...I suggest just adding an open ended "tube" of fabric at either side of the neck...run one corner of ANY blanket up through the tube...knot the corner- instant privacy and the blanket of your choice,- your favorite - more important... baby's favorite! Stash it in the pocket in the back, shown in the photo is one pocket...it could be made bigger, or a second one added. It could all zip together to make another bag...but, I think it is a good way to carry a lot of baby items, and get privacy, because you will wear it...you could walk and breast feed with this. Plus something like this could be used after baby is no longer nursing. In the hot summers I used over my clothing - winters I wore under a jacket. It really does work for most outings! robertacalhoun 5/5/2013
