Clinton Fleenor | 04/26/2013 | 12:15 PM

I worry about the effect of time on the system.

Do names submitted early have an advantage over names submitted with only a day left?

Here's the thing:
Measuring a characteristic like "the community's top ranked name" is like measuring "height".

Example:

Let's say you want to find out who's the tallest person in your class.
So you ask them to let you measure them.
Some of your classmates are cooperative, but most are not. So you decide to "reward" those you don't have to chase down by adding 6 inches to their height.

Now, in the end, have you rewarded those who were cooperative? Sure.
But does the information you collected tell you who is the tallest?
There's no way to know, and that means no.
_____________________________________

If Quirky isn't keeping submission time (plus any other unrelated variables, such as "recognizability") from affecting the results, then it may end up with a top ranked name, but it will have no idea if it was the one the community ranked at the top.
