The Problem

Aquaponics is a wonderful system for growing food (http://www.quirky.com/ideations/469106) but the grow beds you need for raising food take up space. What about people who don't have much room?

The Solution

Part of the wonder of Aquaponics is that half of it is Aquaculture -- and the other half is Hydroponics. You're raising fish -- or lobster, or crawfish, or shrimp, or mussels, or whatever you want you raise -- and using their waste water as plant food in a Hydroponic system. Although it's simplest to lay this out horizontally, we're hardly limited to that axis.

Vertical farming is a known phenomena -- there are even commercial systems like this one on sale already: http://bit.ly/11KCRRe. There are instructions for DIY 'bottle-farms', where you stack 2-liter soda bottles end-to-end, and run hydroponic lines between them -- http://bit.ly/124vK7P. This isn't a new technology that I'm inventing.

What I'm doing is proposing a modular system that hooks into the Quirky Aquaponic (see above) base unit -- a professionally designed and industrially engineered hanging garden. I imagine something that will go on a balcony, but it will probably work in a window as well. All of it hooked into the base unit, providing nutrient-rich water from the Aquaculture component.

Year round, you'll have fresh organic produce, and free fish -- or lobster, shrimp, crawfish, mussels . . . whatever you want to grow. You can even just have pet goldfish.

The only thing is -- this isn't a stand-alone idea. It's an add-on to the base unit. Make sure you vote for http://www.quirky.com/ideations/469106, too!
