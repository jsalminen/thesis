As a musician, I am constantly breaking my instrument cables due to torque. They can cost 50-100+ dollars a cable. I'd like to fix that.

Electronics
Submitted by: Joseph Miller
.

The Problem

Instrument cables are notorious for breaking on tour and during gigs due to torque on the jack and solder joints. Not only that, but this torque causes twisting and tangling of the cable just like old telephone cables used to do (remember those?). And just like those, an adapter built into the cable or separate that rotates 360 degrees relieves this torque saving you from dealing with tangled, twisted cables and from having to buy new cables because yours broke during performance.

The Solution

This 360 degree rotating 1/4" adapter has already been vetted by a IP law firm and there is potential to patent. The device functions by allowing the cable to rotate without putting torque on the solder joints. Further, to avoid threading of the contacts as would be the case for copper or gold, we use a nickle-chromium combination that is both strong and has high conductivity.

Key Features

  • 360 rotation of instrument cables
  • Material that maintain strength and sound quality
