    I like the idea of a glue pen, make it slender like the size of the largest glue stick sold, that would also be able to accommodate/adjust to the smallest glue stick sold. The docking station would be ideal or have the cord at the end of the pen. The extra glue stick station is not a plus for me since I would have additional sticks available when working on the project. The most important is the ease and control of the hot glue. A plastic or medal stand to place the pen on when not in use, and another small stick to spread the glue if so desired. Make is so that it is like a pen that can adjust to the size of the glue stick from small to the largest sold. Most glue guns sold have small cords a cordless glue gun with a docking station is an excellent idea. The trigger could be a small button not necessarily a "trigger" on top or side where you would hold the pen.

    All of these designs are rendered ergonomically unusable, due to the grip thickness, I suggest a major redesign to modify it to be more like a 2.5” painter’s brush handle; the user should be able to apply glue just the same way a painter applies strokes of paint on to a surface, it is a natural back or forward side to side movement of the wrist, allowing the user to apply the glue as desired.
