Bobbie Karchner | 03/17/2013 | 07:12 PM

Hi acgrafix - I am new to Quirky since January, but have tried to spend a lot of time reading the forum and blogs. Steven has said he will give you feedback - Take him up on it, it is a very generous offer. NO, the team does not give feedback on every idea - only the ones that go Under Consideration.
Unless you can explain the idea better or feel you have something new or different, I don't think resubmitting is a good idea. Take Steven's input and make your own decision. I went Pro, since it let me decide without paying for an idea again. When I spoke about making edits, it means to go back to the same idea and hit the edit button. You can change anything at that point. If I add a new feature - I try to include it on my front page, since I want it to catch the attention of the teams looking at the ideas. Votes are nice, but not as important as working on your project to make it the best it can be when the team finally sees it. You still have today to mention in your idea what makes it different - is it more stable, is it more convenient, is it just a better way of doing it? Try to include that on the idea - eg. "You can now use YOUR ladder to change a lightbulb on the stairs" - no expensive equipment to purchase, just use this stable attachment. In your idea be sure to focus on any safety features this will have. Hope that helps - and BTW - I voted. :-)
