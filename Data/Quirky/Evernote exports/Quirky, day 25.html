Watching Feedback Friday on Naming
  • Storyteller and invention ambassador interviewed
  • Process of picking names
    • Team goes through every name after community has voted: 2 invention ambassador, storyteller, Berend, project leader
    • Shortlist
    • Legal checks trademarks
    • Top of list yes, but they check everything just in case to find possible hidden gems
  • Algorithm
    • Not about votes but the ratio
    • Both new and old ideas have same changes
  • Several layers of decision making
  • Process of coming up with names
    • Levels or lenses: culture, brand, category, customer
  • How does the name compare to competition, Quirkys other products, not just what is neat for the product
  • Naming and tagline launched simultaneously, because they need to match
  • Discussing about distinctive names they like
  • Answering questions from audience
    • Algorithm prevents same spelling names to be submitted
    • Rules on different names posted on blog and forum
  • Look at naming brief!
    • No more than 10 s to read it!
    • Three characters to focus on
    • Target group
  • Tagline: a couple of words, not shorter than name
  • Incorporating advice in naming process: compare to design process!!
    • Write down everything that describes the product: target, functions, animal sounds, etc. Get a list of all the factors that go into name. Photos from magazines
    • Select around 20 best
    • Come up with names based on those factors
    • Mix words, borrow from other languages
    • End product: 50-100 names
    • Boil it down to 10-20 names
    • Google search for already registered names
    • Ask outside opinion
    • Select 3 best & send to Quirky for big amount of influence
