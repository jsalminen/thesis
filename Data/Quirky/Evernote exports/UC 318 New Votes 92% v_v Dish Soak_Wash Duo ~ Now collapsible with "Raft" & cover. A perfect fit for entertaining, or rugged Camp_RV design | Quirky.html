The Problem

Maybe there's a better way to do evening dishes. The Collapsible Dish Soak and Wash Duo will allow the kitchen to say uncluttered and organized until later after the fun or to pre-soak for the dishwasher. Many sink drains get broken over time or don't hold water...this designer tub will. This is not a dish drainer...it fits together in the single or double sink. Quirky is working on a different design for a drainer! Created to complement! With a perforated lid as suggested by community, it could also increase counter area and provide a drying space for larger hand washed items, too!

Collaboration with Yuri Saniko

The Solution

The solution: A SOAK and WASH dish rack duo. It fits into one side of a double sink...or in a single large sink that lacks a soaking area. The exterior is a plastic/silicone (collapsible) tub with handle and a "raft" or plug on the bottom which allows the water to go down the drain without lifting the whole heavy tub out of the sink...that's another problem. The plug can lift to open and close back down easily without coming completely out to get lost because it floats! There will be a pull cord attached to the plug, so your hands don't have to search for the plug. The plug is off to one side or on the bottom. The interior rack can hold dishes for a dinner of 6-8 easily and fits nicely inside the tub but allows water to flow through easily and has a SMALL HALF SHELF to put the glasses or cups upright (so they do not get broken in the tub somewhere). Those can also be filled with soapy water to soak...different than shown in illustration. There is a long UTENSIL SECTION for soaking spoons, forks, knives in a downward position. No more hands getting cut on knives!! The area for soaking plates is 8 inches and deeper than shown or they could lie flat completely soaking in the water. A row of small "Ridges" hold the dishes upright. They could also slip easily under the cups, glasses and utensil half shelves. The tub can drain and rack can hold the pre-washed and spray rinsed dishes until dried or ready for the dishwasher. This is sold as a DUO. DOES NOT NEED A DISH DRAINER! Great rugged options also for camping and RV use where space is limited. The removable plug allows easy access to drain tub without lifting it completely out of the sink. Unique for this duo on the market...and will retail nicely with Quirky's new "Raft" floating plug!     

Community Suggestion: Paulette Blankenship: would like a perforated top to cover the unit so the surface could extend into counter space.   More counter space is always welcome in small kitchen spaces! Larger hand-washed items could drain on top, too.

Key Features

  • Colorful plastic/silicone collapsible dish tub with floating drain plug and handles. Tub is to be made higher than the average sink (8-10 inches) increasing the soaking capacity for a small area such as an RV or small apartment. Soaking rack insert also allows easy and safe washing of dishes. Glasses and utensils soak in a half-shelf which doesn't rest on the bottom of the tub.
  • There is space for dishes and bowls to rest /soak upright or lay down in the tub to soak (under the utensil & glass half-shelf). Will hold the dishes for a dinner party of six-eight!
  • This could also be a big hit with campers who enjoy cooking outdoors but not the clean up! Easy soak, wash & rinse with plug outlet. ...let drain to dry in the Duo!
  • The rack itself is also unique because of the half-shelf design and ample utensil area and under space...I love the collapsible option for easy storage of the tub when the full sink area is needed.
  • Colorful and option for Quirky to coordinate with

Product Comparison

  • There are many dish racks and soaking tubs available but not which nest together perfectly in the sink with 2-way soaking / rinsing use & floating drain.
  • The dish pan drain floating plug is a unique feature in the Duo as well the option to keep glasses & cups upright for soaking...handles for easy movement & storage.
  • The color choices allow the Duo to add some jazz to the kitchen sink area...or blend in nicely to be left in place.
  • This could also be the new "must-have" for campers & RV community everywhere!
