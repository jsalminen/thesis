The Problem

FOOD is not supposed to kill you, but more and more, the only thing we can find in grocery-stores are pesticide-ridden Monsanto-grown Frankenfoods. Sure -- the wealthy can afford to pay top-dollar for organics . . . and that's fine, for them.

What about me? What about my family? My neighbors?

Shouldn't OUR children get to grow up strong and healthy, too?

The Solution

This is about FOOD DEMOCRACY. This is about healthy, organically produced and locally sourced produce -- not just FOR everybody, but actually IN everybody's back yard, or on their balcony, patio or basement. This is not high-priced organic food for the wealthy -- this is the modern incarnation of the backyard garden.

It's an established technology -- and it's an ancient one, too -- that is ready to come into the mainstream.

There's any number of plans for DIY-installs, and there's even some pre-made systems designed for home use. The one in the picture above is sold for balcony-use -- and it's priced at more than a THOUSAND dollars . . . and the only thing that's special about it is the nifty one-piece, professionally-designed form factor. Besides that, it's about a eighty dollars worth of parts.

This is a niche market, but it's a niche that's going to rapidly expand in the coming years. As Monsanto comes increasingly into the news, as energy costs continue to cause food-costs to skyrocket, and as study after study continues to show that a diet based on fresh fish and organic produce is the key to longevity, Aquaponics is set to go mainstream.

Where I see Quirky coming in is with design innovation. I imagine a starter-set like the picture I linked, above. It's a one-piece fish tank with an integrated grow-bed and composter. We'll also sell add-ons in the form of additional grow-beds, additional tanks, vertical-column gardens, and hanging bottle gardens.

You start with this basic model, and add on features to create a full garden. Multiple beds, vertical beds, hanging beds -- it'll be an entire system set up turn sunlight into organic food . . . but most of us don't live in Hawaii. Not everybody lives in San Francisco. Most of us live places where it freezes in the winter . . . and that's where the benefits of this system really come into play!

Because it's modular, you can break it back down. The basic unit is splash-safe, and it's a simple thing to reduce the number of fish in the tank, and bring the grow-bed back inside for the winter. When spring comes, expanding a functioning Aquaponic system is much, much simpler than starting one up from scratch. All of the bacteria will be in the right balance as you add on your extra grow-beds and fire the factory back up for the summer!

Key Features

  • Fresh, Organic produce
  • Fresh, Healthy fish
  • Modern, Modular design
  • Energy Efficient, Zero-Emission, Waste-Free Agriculture
