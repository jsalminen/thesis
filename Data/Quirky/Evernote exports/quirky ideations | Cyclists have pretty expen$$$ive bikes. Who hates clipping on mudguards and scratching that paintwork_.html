The Problem

I, like so many cyclists today have a expensive carbon bike - its my baby, my pride and joy. I'm a bit hardcore so I ride no matter what the weather is - to stay competitive you have to do the miles in the wet, if you don't, forget it. But I hate attaching mudguards to my bike. Every mudguard on the market attaches to your bike frame or seat post in one way or the other, and they damage your paintwork or put rub marks on your carbon, and that sucks bigtime.

I have thought up a mudguard idea that doesn't attached to the bike frame, and it only takes seconds to put on without tools. Its super lightweight, the one mudguard fits the rear and the front wheel, and looks freakn cool.

The Solution

My mudguard consists of two main parts, the guard itself and a barrel nut. The barrel nut attaches to the wheel's quick release, locking in place as the quick release is tightened. This also locks the wheel in place. The guard then slides onto the barrel nut to the desired position and fixed using a hex key. Once fitted to suit your bike no further tools are needed to remove or refit - just undo the wheel's quick release and my mudguard comes straight off in seconds.

Anyways my video and website explains all: https://sites.google.com/site/qrguard/video-gallery

  • Its the only mudguard that doesn't touch the bike frame
  • Its the only adjustable mudguard for bunch riding.
