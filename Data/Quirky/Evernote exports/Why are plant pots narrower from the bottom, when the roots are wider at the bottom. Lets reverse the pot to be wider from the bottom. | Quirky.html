Why are plant pots narrower from the bottom, when the roots are wider at the bottom. Lets reverse the pot to be wider from the bottom.

Garden
Submitted by: LuckyAnant
.

The Problem

Why are plant pots narrower from the bottom, when the roots are wider at the bottom.
This in turn means that we are using bigger pots than needs, as the roots are the ones which take the most space.

The second problem is of pots tipping over.

The Solution

I have seen almost straight plant pots, but none which are wider from the bottom and narrower from the top.
There might be such decorative pots but no plant pots which look like this.

Suggestion: Alternately, another solution is to make a cup-holder like item to sit those pots that seem to keep tipping over. This holder would have an open hole at the top equal to the pot, but would get wider at the bottom. This will allow you to still use the pot in a traditional way. Holder would be made of plastic. It will also catch the water for re-using. 

Key Features

  • Reverse pot - Wider from bottom and narrower from top
