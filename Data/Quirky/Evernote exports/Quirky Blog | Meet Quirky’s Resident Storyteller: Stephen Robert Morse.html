Meet Quirky’s Resident Storyteller: Stephen Robert Morse

posted 17 days ago by Nathaniel

Let’s start with this fun fact: As it turns out, Ben Kaufman was born and raised in Oceanside, New York, my hometown on Long Island. Though he’s one year younger than I am, he claims to remember me from those scrappy playground days before he moved away when he was 9. His memory must be way better than mine, because I swear, I don’t remember him at all! That said, I’m thrilled that we have finally met properly now that I’m working here at Quirky as Resident Storyteller!

Working here combines two of my passions: journalism and copywriting. Until March, I worked as Seamless.com’s only copywriter, where I had lots of fun writing about food delivery in a million and one different ways. I created tons of content in many forms, including national ad campaigns, blog posts, and social media content. However, I wasn’t always a copywriter. Before working at Seamless, I worked as a journalist for a range news organizations, including MTV, The Atlantic, Mother Jones, The Huffington Post, The New York Post, Al Jazeera English, The Boston Globe, The Philadelphia Inquirer, and many more, including MyTwoCensus.com, the non-partisan watchdog of the 2010 US Census that I founded and ran. I’ve always had a bit of an entrepreneurial streak in me, and I think that’s what draws me to Quirky. I am amazed every day by how many genius ideas are in our pipeline, and I am fascinated by our production process.

My job here at Quirky is to make your stories come alive. How did you think of your ideas? What makes them so unique and special? Plus, I’m interested in sharing the bigger picture stories of the impact that you’re having on our world. I am also fortunate enough to have lived, studied, and worked in Europe for a solid few years. I was based in Denmark, Amsterdam, England, and Italy! Those experiences, combined with a couple of years after college spent working in California, will hopefully give me an enhanced perspective that I can incorporate into my storytelling.

Don’t hesitate to reach out via email (morsels@quirky.com) or Twitter (@morsels)  if you have questions about naming, taglines, or any other aspects of the Quirky process that I participate in. It’s been a whirlwind first month here at Quirky, and I look forward to working with you!
