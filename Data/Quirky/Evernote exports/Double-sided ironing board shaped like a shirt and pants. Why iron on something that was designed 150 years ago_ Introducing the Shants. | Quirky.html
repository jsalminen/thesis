The Description

The traditional ironing board was originally patented in the 1860s, and for the last 150 years, the product has remained virtually unchanged. But ironing habits and needs have changed dramatically. In the old days, household ironers ironed large items like tablecloths and sheets frequently, requiring the use of a multi-purpose table surface.

Today, most people use ironing boards exclusively for clothing items that need a pressed and polished look, specifically shirts and pants. Enter Shants (SHirts + pANTS = SHANTS): the ironing board contoured to easily fit the items consumers need to iron: shirts and pants.

You can easily “dress” the ironing board with the articles that need ironing and flip the board to iron both sides seamlessly. The Shants will be sized so it can be used across the entire family's wardrobe, with arms and legs that are slightly shorter and thinner than the average man's size. Once the board is dressed, you simply have to pull the sleeve or leg taut over the board's surface if the fit isn't snug.

The Customer

Over 90% of American households currently own a standing ironing board and most of them are frustrated. In a report conducted by Research & Markets, ironing was cited as “the most disliked household task by far,” with consumers deeming it “boring and time-consuming.”

The target consumer for Shants has two distinguishing traits:
- The desire to enhance a tedious experience with a more efficient, more intuitive option
- A sense of humor that welcomes small simple ways to bring joy to their day

The Shants will therefore sit in the comfortable and profitable segment between useful household product and pop culture phenomenon.

Key Features

  • Intuitive shape to match the clothing that the majority of people actually iron.
  • Double-sided design to improve efficiency and cut-down on ironing time (simply place the shirt and pants on the board and flip over after finishing one side).
