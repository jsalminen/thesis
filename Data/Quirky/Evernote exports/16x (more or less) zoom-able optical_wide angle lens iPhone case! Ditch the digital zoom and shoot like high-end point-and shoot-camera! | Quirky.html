The Problem

-The problem with many smart phones is that it doesn't have optical zoom like many dedicated point-and-shoot camera, such as Sony Cybershot DSC-HX9V, which has 16x optical zoom.
-There are other optical zoom iPhone case, but they're all fixed zoom and aren't zoom-able. Plus, it's usually really long and doesn't have wider lens.

The Solution

Let's pretend that this really weird, badly Photoshop large blank iPhone mock-up in a diagram is an iPhone case and here's what I have in mind.

-Have a iPhone case larger than the phone itself to fit the large zoom-able lens to the center of iPhone lens
-Has a dedicated shutter button right on top of volume "+" button and the side button/slider where it controls the optical zoom. For the lens, it would be similar to dedicated point and shoot camera such as Sony Cybershot DSC-HX9V.
-We can either have built in battery to charge the iphone to extend battery life + power the zooming mechanism or we could just have iPhone itself power the zooming mechanism. Either way should work, but I think having battery in it would be even better. I mean, we all want longer battery life, right?

Key Features

  • This could be the world's first zoom-able optical lens iPhone case that acts like high-end point-and-shoot camera.
  • It basically turns iPhone into Samsung Galaxy Camera for higher zoom and wider lens.
