Weekly Recap 4/13 – 4/19

posted 30 days ago by Nathaniel

Another busy week has come to a close, and with it we are checking a ton of new accomplishments off our list. This week we launched a redesigned naming phase and held our first Wink Eval; we picked 17 new Wink ideas and five new ideas at Thursday’s Eval, bringing the total number selected this week to a record breaking 22; and we even found time to launch our new cleaning line to the Upcoming page. But that’s enough prefacing; let’s get down to business. Here’s your weekly recap.

On Monday, notable tech blog Gigaom and Wired’s Gadget lab ran stories on our recent announcement that we’re partnering with Internet-of-Things company, Electric Imp to bring Wink category products to store shelves. On Tuesday, New York startup incubator General Assembly announced their Walk the Web Carpet contest as part of Internet Week in New York City. The contest offers participants the opportunity to win a trip to The Big Apple, during which they can tour notable New York City startups, such as Warby Parker, Fab, and of course, Quirky. Also on Tuesday, The Atlantic ran a piece on inspiring mothers that featured Quirky CEO, Ben Kaufman discussing the importance of his mother, Mindy’s, influence on his development as a business leader. Finally, Gel Conference, an annual speaking event run by customer experience consultancy, Creative Good was held this past Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday, featuring 12 inspiring speakers from various industries, including Ben.

The Electric Imp will make Wink possible

The product teams were extra busy this week, launching sixteen development phases and an entire line of products to the Upcoming page. On Monday, a CMF refine phase was opened for the fruit and veggie toppers, Poppit, and a Community Design phase was launched for the Bluetooth Bracelet. Also launched on Monday were CMF, Naming, and Tagline phases for Butter Twister, and Research phases for the Adjustable Picture Hooks and the Carpentry Scribe Tool. On Tuesday we launched the combination broom and floor duster, Flipside, as well as opened a Research phase for the Double Basin Cake Pan, and Research and Community Design phases for Kitchen Disks. Instead of a Brainstorm, we held our first Wink category Eval on Wednesday, during which we evaluated 30 new app enabled products. On Thursday we launched both the three sided mop, Scrubbie, and the collapsable mop bucket, Nest, and opened a new Packaging Refinement phase for Poppit. We wrapped up the week on Friday with the launch of our new Broom Groomer Pro, and opened branding phases for the Carpentry Scribe Tool and the MacBook Case.

Introducing: Quirky’s new cleaning line

In the blogosphere: we started things off with a behind the scenes look at the development of the dot pattern pen, Ditto and introduced you to our new Wink category partner, Electric Imp. On Monday we gave you a sneak peek at Tuesday’s Gin with Bret Kovacs, Quirky’s Head of Partnerships and Experiential Marketing, and the man behind Wink, and the Naming phase was given a facelift. On Tuesday we gave you the rundown on the new features of the naming and tagline phases, and pumped you up for our spring cleaning themed Feedback Friday. On Wednesday we asked you to help us find past similar ideas for the submissions under consideration for the Wink category prior to Eval, and on Friday we unveiled the rest of our new cleaning line, and published our Invention Picks of the Week from Thursday’s Product Evaluation.

That concludes this week’s recap. We hope you had as much fun this week as we did, and we invite you to come back next week to start all over again. Have a great weekend, and if you are inventing, good luck with you next big idea.
