Brainstorm Aftermath & Recap 5/1

posted 17 days ago by Nathaniel

We held brainstorm an hour early this week, but hopefully most of you were still able to tune in. Three mind bogglingly cool products were discussed last night, including one of our highly anticipated Wink idea, for which members of the Wink team joined the discussion to keep things interesting. If the time change made it difficult for you to catch last night’s broadcast, we’ll get you right as rain with this week’s recap. Let’s dive right in:

Product 526: Collapsible Bike Helmet

Inventor Michael Yoch submitted his idea for a compact, foldable bike helmet in late 2011, and QDS has been hard at work making the idea into an actual product ever since. They brought it to this week’s brainstorm to discuss ways to tweak their current designs and to unearth new features and functionality. If you are a bike enthusiast, check out the dedicated recording of this product brainstorm here.

Product 182: Musical Train Tracks

In late 2012 Ricardo Seola submitted his 2010 iF Concept Award winning idea for musical train tracks to the Quirky community for consideration. Everyone loved it, and this week it was brought to the brainstorm table. The team and their collaborators online discussed ways to expand on Ricardo innovative concept and how end users might interact with it. Watch this discussion on Ustream.

Product 609: Multipurpose Sensor

When Quirky announced its partnership with GE and the introduction of the Wink product category in early April, a surge of new smart-product idea filled the idea scroll. One of these ingenious ideas was for a multipurpose smart sensor, from inventor Denny Fong. Our brainstormers brought in the members of the Wink development team to help hash out potential applications and design challenges for this innovative idea. Watch as they develop this product’s mind map here.
