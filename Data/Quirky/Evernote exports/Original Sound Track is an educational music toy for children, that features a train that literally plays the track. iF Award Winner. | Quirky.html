The Description

Original Sound Track is an educational music toy for children, that features a train that literally plays the track. The toy's track is designed with small bumps on it to pluck the metal keys on the train. The design borrows from the simple mechanics of wind-up music boxes and implements it in a whole new way. Because the track is divided into sections, children can rearrange them to change the song in new ways. The toy would feature multiple track featuring different songs that could also be combined to form even more musical possibilities. Received a iF Concept Award 2010 and has been featured in a huge number of publications since than.
A concept presentation can be found here: http://youtu.be/lhDT0nwjZQM

    The small parts problem is entirely avoidable if the track was flippable and the pegs (dots) were a permanent part of the track: on one side, the pegs engage the tines (tuning forks) on the train, and on the other side they simply point at the floor. They are positioned toward (up) or away (down) from the train by simple pressing, and they stay in position by friction.

    For an entirely different sound, either flip the track or run the train in the other direction, or both. For maximum versatility, every track should be playable in reversed direction relative to the two tracks on either end.

    A row of pegs/dots corresponds to a measure of time; more than one row could be on a segment of track (a beat).

    This whole design may prove to be difficult due to the pegs slowing down the train. One solution is to have the tines / tuning forks on the train be not mechanical, but electro-mechanical, and able to sense the pegs by proximity, not actually touching!
