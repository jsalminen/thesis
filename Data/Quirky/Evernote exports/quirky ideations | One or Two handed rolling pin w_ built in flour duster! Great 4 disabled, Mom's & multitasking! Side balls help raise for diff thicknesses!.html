One or Two handed rolling pin w/ built in flour duster! Great 4 disabled, Mom's & multitasking! Side balls help raise for diff thicknesses!

Kitchen
Submitted by: Jenson

The Problem

When rolling dough, pasta, etc, the way rolling pins are designed it requires 2 hands. Well sometimes we don't have 2 hands to do this. Over Christmas my 1 year old daughter insisted on me holding her while trying to make cookies with my 6 yr old son. Not easy trying to roll dough while holding child. My mom has an illness that has left one hand difficult to use. She loves to bake, but it's impossible for her to use a rolling pin. There are often things we need to do when rolling, like add flour, so we have to stop and put the roller down, add the flour and start rolling again. Paint rollers are made one handed, wouldn't it make sense for rolling pins to be too?

The Solution

Rolling pin that can be used with one hand or 2. The unique design allows for one handed use, or when used 2 handed would utilize more of the upper body strength opposed to relying on arm strength. Flexible balls at either end of roller that can be pushed and locked in certain locations to lift the roller to allow rolling various thicknesses of dough. Built in flour duster makes process quicker and easier that can be sprinkled or proofed on by pressing.

Key Features

  • Can use one handed
  • Use more upper body strength vs arm strength when using two hands
