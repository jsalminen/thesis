Young Inventors at the Old Tappan Invention Convention

posted 28 days ago by baron

Behind every great inventor lies a great teacher. Whether it’s a parent, educator, or even a supportive friend, every great mind needs someone to guide them; to show them how to brainstorm and problem-solve; to tell them that there’s no reason they can’t make their ideas a reality. That was the thought behind the Invention Convention, a semester-long program held each year at T. Baldwin Demarest Elementary School, in Old Tappan, NJ. Led by enrichment teacher Andrea Mishler, students are walked through a gradual development process quite similar to Quirky’s: they brainstorm problems, test their solutions through prototyping, and present the results of their work at a climactic final assembly.

This year, the Invention Convention was poised to be bigger than ever, and at the suggestion of Quirky videographer David Mishler, the staff decided to get involved and see what the program was all about. A team of Quirky staffers took a trip to Old Tappan to give a presentation on invention, after which a group of students came to QHQ for a tour and a workshop with the design staff.
