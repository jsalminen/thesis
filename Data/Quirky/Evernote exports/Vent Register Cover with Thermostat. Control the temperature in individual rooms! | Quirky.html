Vent Register Cover with Thermostat. Control the temperature in individual rooms!

Electronics
Submitted by: MichaelS
.

The Problem

In a house with multiple rooms or floors it can be difficult to get heating and air conditioning to heat or cool the house evenly. Our current house has 2 floors served by a single HVAC unit with one thermostat. During the winter the upper floors get too much heat and during the summer the lower floors get too much air conditioning. This means we are constantly tweaking the vent register flaps to try to direct the air into different rooms, and we are heating or cooling rooms when we aren't using them.

The Solution

It would be great to have a vent cover with a thermostat that would open and close to keep the temperature steady in that room. Rooms that would normally get too hot during the winter would automatically close off the vents when they were the right temperature, forcing the heat to go to other rooms. This could be a magnetic cover that sticks on your current vent registers.
These could have a simple thermostat, or they could follow the model created by the Nest Thermostat. That thermostat is great for small houses as it monitors activity in the room and turns itself down if nobody is home. It is less useful in a large house where people might not enter the room where the Nest thermostat is located, but be active in other parts of the house. A more complex version of the vent register cover could have activity monitors and close off when nobody is in the room, or be programmable with a schedule. This would help you save energy by only heating or cooling the rooms you are in. Our bedrooms are empty during the day, but the living areas are busy. At night it is the opposite. Controlling the air flow would save a lot of energy. There are louver systems that can be installed on HVAC systems, but those are not very consumer friendly. We need something the average person can just stick on their vent register for any room they want to control.

Key Features

  • Helps control the temperature in individual rooms
  • Could save money by shutting off vents when rooms aren't used

Product Comparison

  • Manual flaps on vent covers
  • Nest thermostat (not room specific)

    Adjusting vents in a house is a good idea conceptually. In office buildings it's called air balancing and services get good money to do this but the end result is a stationary setting for each vent. You definitely don't want a situation where the main thermostat calls for heat and all your vent thermostats shut down the air. That's likely to happen because not all thermostats are created equal. Another problem is that you need to design hysteresis into your control system or you'll end up with oscillating vents when you reach your target. Each vent state (open, shut, or in between) and each vent temperature sensor should to be monitored and possibly controlled by the main thermostat. They should not all run autonomously. How do you intend to power these vents? Batteries probably wont last long and running off AC presents its own problems. You could use HomePlug to give you an easy communications link over the houses AC between vent controls and the thermostat. Well, maybe not so easy.
    It's a tough problem but if you crack this nut it'll sell like crazy. Good luck.
