@Jason - I'm familiar with how Reddit works, and if you've been around there long enough, then you'll know that short comments like "I like this." or "I don't like this." are down voted because they are of little value to the community as you've stated. A great comment/feedback, whether it be positive or negative, is the thing that thrives at Reddit and are constantly pushed up to the top.

The game you're suggesting here is very much already in place... A member can look at a submission and comment "I like this because" and "I don't like this because"... you would just kind of be forcing them into at least a character minimum. However, I don't see how this encourages the members here to even play the game, which is the problem; that most submissions don't get enough feedback.

Take away the incentive in the vote. Take away the big blue vote button all together. Make the incentive in the feedback. When it's all done, submissions should have a greater amount of feedback than they're receiving now, even if they never make it to UC. You can reward all feedback that you deem constructive in the process, whether it be positive or negative.

Votes are an easy way for Quirky to divide that "Winning Votes" influence but it's also killing what you actually want members to do... which is participate other than within their own submissions.

I found the right place for a comment I made earlier. Hi. I've been around for three days. Sorry about all the toes I've stepped on. I'll find my way around soon.
.
Back to the subject: My inclination would be to employ both a Thumbs Up and a Thumbs Down. Thumbs up would not require any comment. It's implied that yes, this idea shows promise. We should spend some more time on this.
.
Thumbs down would require a reason, such as:
Gandolf doesn't work here____
This product already exists____ (and here's a link)___
Nobody would buy this at the price it would take to build it__
Fill in the blank________
.
Ideas are presented from highest Up:Down ratio, with newer ideas randomly mixed in. New ideas would stay in the stream until 100 or so people have seen them. After that, they take their place in the hierarchy. First impressions mean a lot, and it would behoove the originator to spend some time on polishing the presentation. An idea might get voted down, but at least the originator would have a good idea why it didn't rise, and suggest how to resurrect the idea. The originator may withdraw the idea, make changes, and resubmit without waiting 30 days (for an appropriate fee, of course.)
.
Many, many ideas currently get submitted by "Pros" with no thought, no research, and suggest building some kind of magic-based item. With Thumbs Up/Down, those would go to the bottom very quickly, meaning few of us would ever be afflicted by these time-wasters.
.
I'd also like to see a Cred rating next to people's names. You get Cred if people "Thumbs Up" your comments (in the Evaluate New Ideas section and in the Forums.) You also get Cred when people give you props on your profile page.
.
Ideas submitted by people with more Cred are given hierarchy over another idea with an equivalent Up/Down ratio.
.
You can Thumbs Down a comment, but then you must say why. Down voting a comment lowers the original poster's Cred, but it lowers your own by the same amount. The original poster is more likely to take the advice given knowing "This hurts me as much as it hurts you." Just as multiple people can give a post a Thumbs Up, multiple people can also give a Thumbs Down. With this system, people would be much less likely to be critical in a harsh or thoughtless way if they know they can lose Cred for abusive comments. I suspect mean or poorly worded comments would be removed quickly by the original poster.
.
Off subject: One more things that needs to be addressed is: if someone P.M's you, as it is, you are unable to reply unless they are "Following" you.
