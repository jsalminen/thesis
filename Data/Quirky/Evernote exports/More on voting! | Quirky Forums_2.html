More on voting!

As a part of our initiative to increase visibility to your ideas, and amplify your voice, we are considering overhauling the current 15 vote per diem system in combination with Under Consideration status.

Should your idea acquire 100+ votes, it will automatically be put Under Consideration. And you will have 5 votes instead of 15.

I think it's a solid idea. That way the Q staff can spend less time digging through the sea of submissions and more time focusing on the ones deemed worthy by the community. It would leverage the crowdsourcing concept much more effectively than the current system, which places the entire onus of idea selection on the relatively small Q staff.

Surely people will try to game the system. Don't put it past individuals to create 100 different Q accounts. But some basic IP monitoring can take care of that.

As for popularity contests, they won't last long if the staff consistently separates the UC wheat from the chaff.

What this begs though is a new, more expansive interface -- something Pinterest-like -- with ability to show in chronological/random/etc. order. Scrolling down in a single column can get pretty tedious. A 3 X 3 or 4 X 4 grid would really reduce the time required to sift through ideas. 4 X 4 would allow a rapid scan of 16 ideas, whereas I can only see a max of 4 at once with the current design. 4-fold increase in review speed = more coverage of idea subs by the community. The effect would be similar to that of the new ELO system for naming.
