Feedback on idea voting

Hi community - My name is Jim and I am a designer on the Quirky Platform team. The next piece of the Quirky Platform that we are updating is the idea voting interface. Our motto is "better,faster,funner" and that's what we want for idea voting!


I have built out two simple prototypes and would love to get feedback on them. Note that these are *very* rough and are just simple tests right now. The final versions will look much better:


Full Screen View
3 Across View


For each page, pretend that you have just 5 votes to use out of a total of 30 ideas. Just click on the idea to vote. After you play with both interfaces, it would be awesome to hear your thoughts both on this forum and in this survey:
Survey page


Also, if anyone would like to chat 1-on-1 about voting on ideas and ways to make the interface better, feel free to email me at jim@quirky.com :)

Kansas | 04/20/2013 | 04:05 PM

Jim ... these are just my initial thoughts based on what I'm seeing.
.
Full Screen View (permalinks) - Some entries just aren't worth a click. I would not review or vote on entries if I had to view them in this format. (Just being completely honest) However, this would make a great start on a permalink page to link to from an index page.
.
Three Across View (index) - Much better! However, I would probably want to view the permalink with a little more info on some (not all) before casting a vote. The short summary in addition to the title is really nice though. Also I'm not sure that we need the submitter's name on any of the entries - it really shouldn't be a consideration when reviewing/voting. I'm cool with blind submissions. Maybe they still show up on people's profiles though?
.
You may want to check out NOTCOT.ORG - there are a lot of features that I think could be applicable here. I like the full screen grid because it means less scrolling. And for some reason I really like the idea of a 'like' and 'dislike' button over a 'vote' button. Also I like the way the site has been set up using a series of mini sites instead of one big site - it's kind of like having channels. Also a paginated navigation may be easier to use than an endless scroll when it comes to picking up where you left off.
.
On a side note ... I am not in agreement with giving the community any options to censor any submissions at all - the community can't even submit proper comp shops. I think it would send the wrong message to new people and possibly discourage them right off the bat. There is a learning curve involved here ... I think some of the old-timers need to be a little more patient and understanding. Eventually the new people will get it figured it out.
