        As I stated in my comment on the "Dock Storage and Adjustable Nozzles" design, hot glue is not cookie dough or cake icing. I don't see the point of using different nozzles. A thin line is usually best, as hot glue tends to be used on one small area at a time. The thin line of glue invariably widens as it oozes, and the glue only remains workable for 15 seconds or so. I really don't see the point of different nozzles.

        On the other hand, this design would work nicely for cake decorating. (Seriously!)

          I like the multi-nozzle idea, but the problem is that when you are done glueing and unplug it, the glue will harden inside the nozzle head. The next time you go to use it, you will not be able to turn the nozzle head until you let it get hot enough to melt the internal glue, and then you can turn the head, but the glue left in the other nozzles will probably leak-out. If you could find a way to overcome this limitation and make the nozzle head much smaller, then this would be a real nice multi-purpose design.
