Sensory Overload: An Update on Our Wink Eval

posted 24 days ago by justin

A primary goal of our Wink: Instantly Connected product line is to build devices that think for you, rather than devices that you need to think about yourself. Whether you’re in your kitchen or on the go, your devices should “know” what’s going on around them, letting you worry about other, more important things. We picked Denny Fong‘s multipurpose sensor for this very purpose: with its simple form and boundless versatility, we felt that this idea submission truly captured the essence of the connected home.

However, after our special Wink Eval last Wednesday, we learned that Denny had submitted the same concept for home sensors over a year ago, long before the creation of Wink. As we researched Denny’s concept further, we uncovered some other great concepts for “home sensors” that fit within the Wink category as well.

Thus, we’re excited to announce that Denny’s idea for home sensors has been split into five separate products, each with a distinct approach to Denny’s original sensor concept. While three of the product applications were outlined by Denny in his submissions, two other home sensor products were picked from the archive of previously submitted ideas.

So, congratulations to the multiple inventors who will now be crowned from a single idea submission! In addition to Denny, we’re also crowning Sarah Streitwieser for her mailbox sensor idea submission, and Marc Rumaner for his laundry sensor.

Keep submitting your ideas, and we’ll keep working on ways to connect you with the important things in life. Our next special Wink Eval is coming up on May 21st at 7pm ET, so mark your calendars now, and keep your eyes on the blog for more updates!
