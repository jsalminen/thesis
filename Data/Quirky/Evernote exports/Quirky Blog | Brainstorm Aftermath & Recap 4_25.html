Brainstorm Aftermath & Recap 4/25

posted 24 days ago by Nathaniel

We know you missed Brainstorm last week, so naturally we wanted to make this week’s extra awesome. Three exciting new electronics products were discussed and we invited our resident electrical engineers, Nathan Meryash and Tracy Van Dyk, to lend expertise. If you missed any of the action, we’re happy to get you up to speed with this week’s recap. Let’s get to it, shall we?

Product 461: LiliLite

Thijs Smeets submitted this idea for an all encompassing reading accessory, that combines a book shelf, bookmark, and reading light into a single product. QDS and friends discussed form, placement, and how to simulate the full reading experience through the final design. You can see what came out of this discussion here.

Product 561: Electromagnetic Workbench

Zaccheus Miller‘s idea for a workbench that uses electromagnets to generate variable resistance was so compelling we Evaled it not once, but twice. The second time around Zaccheus sent us a prototype, which we tested under his supervision during Eval. Our brainstorm team discussed functionality, feature feasibility, and other insanely cool possibilities that you can watch for yourself here.

Product 512: Segmenting Flashlight

Back in November, Julio C Del Castillo submitted his idea for a versatile, rechargeable flashlight, that could break into individual mini lights. The Quirky community loved the idea, pushing it through Eval and landing it on last night’s brainstorm. Our intrepid brainstormers mapped out how the final product could work, how to expand on the core idea, and what the key use cases are. Check out the discussion here.
