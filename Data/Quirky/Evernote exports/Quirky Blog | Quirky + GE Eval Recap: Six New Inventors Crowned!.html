Quirky + GE Eval Recap: Six New Inventors Crowned!

posted 5 days ago by stephen

Tim Gunn and Corinne Iozzio joined Ben Kaufman at our special Eval last night.

Last night, we hosted our second special product evaluation with our friends at GE, focusing on app-enabled products. We had Project Runway’s Tim Gunn, Popular Science magazine’s Corinne Iozzio, GE’s Joe Salvo and Deborah Zajac, and Electric Imp’s co-founder Kevin Fox on our special panel that resulted in some great news: We crowned six new Quirky + GE inventors!

Here’s a run-down of the winning app-enabled inventions:

Our first inventor of the night was Blinky, who wants to create an app-powered sprinkler, which we think will be great for whenever we’re on vacation, or when it’s raining too much or too little.

Next up, was one for the kids and kids at heart: An app-powered digital scorekeeper that lets you instantly transform any standard basketball hoop into a digital score counter. We also discussed applying this technology to other sports that require nets too. Congrats to inventor David Veshapidze!

We hope that coffee drinkers will get excited for what our Wink apps will be able to do for them: Mo Herzig wanted to create an app-powered coffee machine, and we did too! We also think that this may sync up well with the app-powered alarm clock that we’re already making!

Who didn’t love having a globe in your bedroom as a kid? And how much cooler would that globe be if it could highlight where mom or dad was when they were away on a business trip? Enter Globe 2.0. We loved this concept and we’re so happy that MarkK submitted it!

The next idea came from Mike Kildevaeld, who definitely doesn’t want to schlep to his front door every time the doorbell rings. We agreed that there would be an awesome app-enabled solution for this problem: Enter the front door peephole equipped with a camera that shoots a photo of whomever’s at the door and sends it over to your iPhone. Score!

Our final invention came from Kansas, who wanted to be able to track what we store in boxes with the tap of our app. We thought this was a great idea that could have some major practical applications both at home and for businesses.

Huge congrats to all of our inventors, and be sure to submit your app-enabled ideas here.
