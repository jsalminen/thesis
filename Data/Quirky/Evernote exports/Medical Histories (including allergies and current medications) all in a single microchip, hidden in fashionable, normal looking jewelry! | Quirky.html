The Description

Easier, quicker, ways to transfer medical history in emergency situations - and not have to wear a traditional "allergy" bracelet!

The Customer

Jewelry with small microchips containing downloadable information. Earings, bracelets, rings, even nose rings and belly button rings that have emergency medical information that, when scanned, can tell a doctor a persons allergies, health history, current medications, and even insurance information! Easier than trying to wait for medical records, medication list, ect when time is limited!

    Hi Hannah, It's an interesting idea. I'm not trying to be mean here but I have some questions. Are you saying that this would be something you would wear when you go to a doctors office and can tell them you have this chip? If so, that would mean that the doctors office would have to have a scanner or something to read it. There are USB bracelets that can store medical info that doctors can read via USB but it's not a really established practice, this would be even less established.

    If you are saying it would be useful if you were in an accident and had to go the ER I am wondering how an ER doc would know you have the chip in a piece of jewelry that is not tagged as a "Medical" piece of jewelry. Is an ER doc going to realize your belly or nose ring has your medical history stored on it? Since this isn't a standard that has been established, they would not know to look for it and would probably not have the equipment in place to scan for the chip. Plus all medical offices and ER's would have to invest in some type of scanner, that's a big investment for something that does not have an established protocol.

    Again, I am not trying to be a jerk or be mean but these are real world issues that would arise and I am wondering how you see this working in actual practice.
