Weekly Recap 4/20-4/26

posted 23 days ago by Nathaniel

It’s that time of the week again, makers: time to kick off your shoes and reflect on everything that transpired over the last seven days. If you missed anything in the Quirkyverse this week, you’ve come to the right place. Here’s your weekly recap to get you up to speed.

Other than a hilarious Buzzfeed article loosely about our partnership with GE, it was a quiet week for Quirky in the press. All the better, because it allowed us to focus on making products! On Monday we launched a community design phase for Mixing Claw, as well as design and research phases for the Mac Mini Flip Stand, and CMF, Naming, and Tagline phases for theDouble Basin Cake Pan. Early Tuesday afternoon we launched the iPad keyboard case, Rollback, to the Upcoming page, which was followed later in the day by a community design phase for the 3-in-1 Gift Wrapping Tool, and a new retail research phase for Bake Shapes. On Wednesday we opened a community design phase for the Mini Rolling Measurement Tool and a research phase for the 3-in-1 Gift Wrapping Tool,  and that evening QDS brainstormed the all-in-one bookshelf, LiliLite, the Electromagnetic Workbench, and the Segmenting Flashlight. In addition to a very successful Eval on Thursday night (more on that later), we launched the butter measuring and spreading tool, Butter Boss, and opened another CMF phase for Trek Support. We rounded out the week on Friday by launching the spotlight fan, Airlight, to the Upcoming page, and opening a CMF phase for Kitchen Disks.

Introducing: Airlight

As far as the Quirky blogosphere is concerned, you couldn’t have asked for a better week. Baron started us off early on Monday with a piece about our visit with young inventors at T. Baldwin Demarest Elementary School in Old Tappan, New Jersey, and with a new Monday Design Tip about the innovative potential of biomimicry. On Tuesday, we tweeted the new Quirky.com sketch application, which lets community members draw their own stick figure avatars, and Baron gave us the lowdown on new flexible touch screens that are poised to change the world of interface design. Later on Tuesday, Stephen Morse introduced the Quirky community to the founders of our Wink collaborator, Electric Imp, and Baron interviewed Quirky’s Head of Community, Steven Shaw in the latest episode of Gin with…. On Wednesday, Wink inventors everywhere received a few welcome pointers on developing instantly connect product ideas from Stephen Morse, and Baron took us behind the scenes of development for our upcoming utility shovel, Zeus. GE project manager, Justin Seidenfeld announced on Thursday that we are splitting multipurpose sensor inventor Denny Fong’s idea into five distinct products, and that we are crowning two new inventors – Sarah Streitwieser for her mailbox sensor and Marc Rumaner for his laundry sensor – whose products are part of this new sensor line. Also on Thursday we announce a live chat Q&A in lieu of a Feedback Friday broadcast, and on Friday we announced the four new inventors crowned in Thursday’s product evaluation.

With that, we bid you adieu for this last full week of April. Have a great rest of your weekend and try to get out into the sunshine if you have it. Normally we reserve this final sentence for inviting you to watch our Feedback Friday broadcast and a recording of Gin with…, but there was none of the former, and Baron already beat us to the latter in his live chat Q&A announcement. Oh what the heck, enjoy Gin with… below if you missed it. We promise it’s well worth your time to watch.
