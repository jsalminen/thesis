Submitted by: John Rakowski
 
 

The Description

A grabber/reacher that has more options.I want something that will make my mom,as well as others like her more independent.CHECK OUT KEY FEATURES BELOW.I know this is possible with Quirky & the community putting or heads together.Lets make this idea as unique & as cool possible,so everyone will want one. Several areas of reinvention that would be quite useful would be to give the claw the ability to 1.(rotate) and 2.(bend) with respect to the attached arm.  This would provide greater dexterity while grabbing objects and eliminate the contorting that people must go through in order to make the best contact with items they must grab.  The ability to 3.(expand or collapse) would also be valuable especially for portability and travel.

 

The Customer

I see this product used by the elderly,people who have problems bending or anyone in need of grabbing things beyond there grasp.Everyone wants to feel independent.I feel Quirky & the community together can design a more updated & versatile grabber with the suggested key features below.Invented by:John Rakowski.

 

Key Features

  • Small battery operated LED light at end of grabber.Something falls behined or underneath /BED or CABINET
  • A small high powered neodymium magnet at very tip of grabber.Help pick up small or large metal objects
  • Added arm support /as in pictures
  • Make extendable to reach in higher places
  • Maybe even a small shoe horn
  • But still make it sturdy and light weight as possible

    I particularly like the idea of the adjustable arm support to assist weak wrists, but would suggest this be made as a reach-thru sleeve, instead of the pictured U shape. The trigger handle design would work best with this feature. The other designs pictured pretty much require two hands, which is a problem for those needing the use of their other arm for standing assistance/support. As an owner/manager of an Elderly Home, I have witnessed a number of problems with these devices due to non-pivoting/non-rotating pinchers which tend to cause spillages when moving items from higher locations or even lifting things from the ground. The suction cup grabbers (not pictured) work better for this and are able to pick up irregular shaped objects as they conform to the shapes better. They can even pick up coins! Perhaps a better design would use ball bearings at the end of each `finger' and be attached to a tacky, mallable, cupped, plastic-like substance that would allow a lower center of gravity of an object to keep the contents upright and level when pinched as it would permit the object to pivot between the fingers. Also, consider some method to `lock' the pinchers to relieve the stress on arthritic hands as someone else suggested.

    Great, Lawrence, nothing like hearing from the voice of experience.

    As for the extending and positioning, I think some scheme like they use for extending camera tripod legs would be good. If twist locking is a problem, they do have those small lever locks. This would allow the user to lock in the length and rotational position before using them.

    The shoe horn use would require 3-4 inch offset in the handle since it is tough for most ppl to reach back behind themselves. This offset could be incorporated in the shoehorn attachment.

    Lawrence's suggestion of a full "cuff" support makes good sense, and I am thinking you could add a great deal of stability by having a sliding extension to come under and a bit back up onto the back of the upper arm. This would allow the much stronger shoulder muscles to add power and stability.

    This extension could be retracted or fully removed.

    Also, this elbow extension would relieve the hand and wrist from maintaining or achieving a rotational load.

    Any optional attachments and adjustments would have to be user friendly to those with limited finger movement and hand strength.
