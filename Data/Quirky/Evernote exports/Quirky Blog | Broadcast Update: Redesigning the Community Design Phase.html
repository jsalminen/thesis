Broadcast Update: Redesigning the Community Design Phase

posted 4 days ago by baron

If you’ve been keeping up with our Quirky Live broadcasts, you’ve probably noticed that the last few weeks have had a focus on naming, to build on the success of our brand new naming platform. Now that the system is up and running, we’d like to move in a new direction, and focus on another part of the Quirky site we’re hoping to improve: specifically, the Community Design Phase.

That’s right folks, the team that brought you “This or That” naming has turned their gaze towards our collaborative design platform, and we want to make sure that the updated tool is as awesome for community members as it is helpful for our designers. To start things off on the right foot, we’re hosting a special episode of Feedback Friday, this Friday at 3pm ET, dedicated exclusively to a revamp of the Community Design Phase.

Our panel will include invention ambassadors Anna Buchbauer and Andrew Erlick, along with designer Aaron Saxton. All three work closely with the current platform, and will be able discuss what the Quirky staff want to get out of the overhaul (more influence, better staff guidance, and more robust submissions are all on the list). However, there are two sides to every coin, and we’re dying to get some community input as well, which is why it’s so essential that you join in yourself!  Anything and everything is up for discussion, from the structure of the Community Design Phase to the amount of influence distributed, so if you participate in design projects on a regular basis, we recommend you tune in.

So how can you get involved in this very special symposium? Just head to Quirky.com/live this Friday at 3pm ET, and be ready with your questions! Alternatively, you can post your questions in the comments section below, and we’ll share them on the air. In the meantime, check out this recording of last week’s Feedback Friday for a better idea of what you can expect.
