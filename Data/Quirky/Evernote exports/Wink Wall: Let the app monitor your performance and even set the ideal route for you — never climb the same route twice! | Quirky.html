Wink Wall: Let the app monitor your performance and even set the ideal route for you — never climb the same route twice!

Wink
Submitted by: simplyinclined
.

The Problem

Endless climbing walls have been around for a while - and they are pretty much the state-of-the art for safe, program based climbing practice.

On the standard walls, you can set a route (in advance) by moving the climbing blocks but it takes forever since each block has an allen bolt that needs to be unscrewed and replaced once the block is moved.

They have limited controls during use. You can adjust the rate of climb as you go, by pushing control levers, and the device will auto-brake for safety when your feet get below a certain point... but that is as far as they go.

Today's climber needs an app-enabled endless wall that can monitor the user and their performance and can set the right route and the right speeds based on the desired program. It needs to be able to surprise and challenge the user it needs to be able to do that for every person in the family who wants to use it.

The Device

The device is an endless roller shutter with climbing blocks. Generally it would work like other endless climbing walls but the biggest difference would be that, instead of the blocks being bolted to the faces of planks, the would be positioned on moving runners at the joints.

The joints would be moved by a carriage as simple as the one in an inkjet printer. The movement would happen when the joints were 'opened out' at the top of the device. The blocks would be held tight as the joints closed up again .

An iPhone iPad or other intelligent device could be used to monitor the climber and provide feedback

Patents. Please note that the patents for this type of device only seem to cover particular braking and hinging methods - the overall design does not appear to be protected. By all means look to see if I have missed something, but if you are adding to the comp-shop, please do not add any of these limited patents.

App Features

  • Creates and endlessly variable route
  • Monitors the performance of multiple users
  • Adjusts based on feedback from the camera in the iPad/iPhone etc
  • Greatly enhances the practice
