A better tie down ratchet allowing you to choose position, and gives twice the tension. Resub includes improved cam design.

Other
Submitted by: Chris Howard
.

The Description

New cam design features a drawbridge style cam making it easier to place on the webbing plus improved cam mechanism. This would replace the need for the cable as seen in the first submission.

No more poorly placed ratchet buckle to scratch your precious cargo. My new cargo ratchet buckle design can be placed anywhere on the strapping to suit your cargo or tie down requirements. The new design means the ratchet buckle handle is pulled down to tension, not up like traditional ratchets giving better leverage for greater tension and tactile feel. Because the tying of the strapping follows the same method as a Truckers Knot, the same 2 to 1 purchase principles apply meaning you get double the tie down force on your cargo for the same effort as any other ratchet. Watch my video to see how it works at... or see below... http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1s1FCV5fiNY

My ratchet would come in the usual variety of sizes, lengths, strap widths, locking features, rated strengths and colors. Could be sold as single units or in multiples of 2 or 4 sets.

The Customer

Cargo ratchets are widely used and sold through many markets around the world such as timber & hardware stores, camping & adventure stores, fishing, boating and 4 wheel drive.

Key Features

  • Can remove ratchet from strapping.
  • Ratchet can be placed anywhere on the strapping to suit your cargo.
  • Strapping can be replaced when worn without having to buy another ratchet handle.
  • Truckers Knot tying principle doubles the user's tie down force
  • Ratchet is inverted resulting in tension created by pulling down on handle, not up giving greater leverage.
  • Minimal tooling needed as only the lower section is changed
  • The design change could be applied to any current ratchet design

Product Comparison

  • Any standard ratchet buckle
  • Cam buckles.
  • Rope tie down tools such as the TiteTie, Figure 9 and Rope Ratchet.
  • Elastic cords
