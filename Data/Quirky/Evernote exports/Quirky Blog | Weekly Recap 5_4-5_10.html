Weekly Recap 5/4-5/10

posted 4 days ago by Nathaniel

Ah the weekend: two blissful days for rest, relaxation, and getting caught up on all things Quirky. If you missed out on any of the development phases we opened, or products we introduced to the Upcoming page, you need not look further than this post for an update. Hold onto your butts, because here comes the weekly recap.

Our partnership with GE continued to generate buzz this week, with a new piece from Popular Mechanics about the potential for the Wink line to reinvent home appliances. Quirky community member and burgeoning Edison in his own right, Len Nannarone, was recently featured in Wired for working with his dad to submit an idea for the next app-enabled golf swing analyzer to Quirky. Colorado’s 9News included Pen Zen, Crossover and Bobble Brush Timer in their gadget roundup, and Carolina Now ran a gift guide segment that included Tether as something special for mom. The Celebrity Cafe applauded Grill Wrangler’s innovative design and PC Mag published a roundup of best iPhone 5 cases that included Crossover. On Friday, journalist and Google enthusiast, Jeff Jarvis, recounted his experience sitting on Quirky’s Eval panel this past week to his Google+ followers.

PC Mag loves Crossover

We had a crazy busy week of new development projects and launches to the Upcoming page. On Monday the Invention Ambassadors threw open the doors by launching not one, not two, but ten (ten!) phases for the Quirky community to participate in. The day was started with the launch of naming and tagline projects for the Mac Mini Flip Stand, followed by CMF phases for the Mixing Claw, the Mini Rolling Measurement Tool, the 3-in-1 Gift Wrapping Tool, Rake-n-Tamp (CMF 2 actually), and the Smart Piggy Bank; and research phases for three Wink ideas: the Mailbox Alert Sensor, Laundry Alert, and the Multi Purpose Sensors. On Tuesday we introduced the world to the collapsible kitchen tools, Snapware, and opened a research phase for the rocking teapot, Rock Pot, in addition to starting both a survey and a community design project for the Musical Train Tracks. Wednesday was the day we launched research phases for the Wink Refrigerator Camera and Holiday Lights, and that evening we brainstormed the Refrigerator Camera, the Smart Alarm Clock, and the Door Camera. On Thursday we opened a refine project for the Collapsible Bike Helmet and launched the bluetooth bracelet phone, Mingle, to the Upcoming page. Finally, on Friday we introduced Prop Power Rugged to the Upcoming page, opened a new research phase for RotoBlade, and invited the Quirky community to compare names for the Smart Piggy Bank.

Prop Power Rugged is the latest addition to the Upcoming page

In Quirky blog news: Baron kicked off the week with his celebrated Monday Design Tip. In this installment he made the case for Raspberry Pi, the $25 mini circuit board that is perfect for electronics hacking and prototyping electronic inventions. That same day he gave the Quirky community a sneak peak at this weeks Gin with industrial designer Julia Troy. On Tuesday we received an update on a special Feedback Friday about crafting the perfect product name, as well as a preview of Wednesday’s brainstorm. We announced that we’ll be making Cyclone and posted our interview with twelve time Quirky inventor, Marc Zech, on Wednesday, and on Friday we wrapped up the week with the invention picks from Thursday’s Eval.

That’s what happened in the Quirkyvers during this second week of May. We hope you had as much fun catching up as you did participating (if you had the opportunity), and can’t wait to start anew with you on Monday. Have a great rest of your weekend and remember to keep inventing! Here are the recordings of Gin with Julia Troy and Feedback Friday for your viewing pleasure.
