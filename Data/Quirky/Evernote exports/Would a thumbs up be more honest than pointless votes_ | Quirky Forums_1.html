Michael Grimshaw | 04/17/2013 | 11:34 PM

The $10.00 fee helps, for sure. The Pros are abusing the privilege. I'm partial to the Up/Down ratio - not because I suggested it (I bet I wasn't the first) but because it solves the over-posting problem and the not-thought-out problem and the unfairness of posting when the website has many visitors.

Ranking by Up/Down ratio means if it's a bad idea, or if it's impossible, or if it's already a product, or for any other reason, the idea will sink. A statistically significant number of people will see the idea, and if it's ready to build, it will go to the top. If it needs work, it will go to the middle. If it's the spam of a Pro, it will go to the bottom.

This system works no matter how many submissions Quirky gets in a day, or how many people are online that day. Good ideas go up, bad ideas go down. The ideator can resubmit as many times as they want, but he (or she) is going to get almost exactly the same results every time.

When ideas are improved, they will get immediate feedback that the work they've put into it is making a difference.

Solves SO many perennial and persistent problems. Does anyone see a down side?
