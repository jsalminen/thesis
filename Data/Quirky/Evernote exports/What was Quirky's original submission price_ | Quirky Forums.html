@Kreg, Jake submitted when it was $99.

Back then it was worth that, though.
While Jake's submission was only good for one week, and the system was more of a competition since Q only picked one idea a week, Jake was only competing with maybe 30 other submissions.

(BTW, back then resubmissions were only $10...and most ideas were selected on the second or third try. Pivot Power was a first try, though.
Also, the fee changed to $10 in the fall of 2010 - at about the same time as the number of submissions started to hit about 100+ - but the system remained a one week contest.
The free "briefs" [Q telling us what they wanted] were added then too, making the Q system two contests a week.
The system changed to the current 30 day non-contest in the fall of 2011.)
