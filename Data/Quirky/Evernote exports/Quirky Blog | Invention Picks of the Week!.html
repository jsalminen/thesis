Invention Picks of the Week!

posted 30 days ago by baron

No, you’re not experiencing déjà vu. After our first ever Wink Eval took place this Wednesday, last night marked the second Quirky product evaluation in as many days, capping off the week with a record high 23 new inventions. Six of those were picked at last night’s event, so without further ado, let’s take a look at the newest crop of Quirky inventors:

The first pick of the night came from Stem inventor Timothy Houle, who expanded his fruity dynasty with a fun and functional watermelon pop maker. Similar to an apple corer, the device cuts a cylinder of juicy goodness directly out of the fruit, creating a fun and unique way to eat melon that also cuts down on the mess. The Eval crowd praised the concept for its creativity and versatility, proposing that it could be used for other fruits as well. Heck, if the success of Stem is any indicator, Tim may be on his way to reinventing fruits entirely.

The next invention came from Neil MacAloney, who submitted a skin-sensing utility knife that would retract its blade as soon as it made contact with skin. Neil’s concept is based on the same technology as the SawStop table saw, and while some audience members questioned whether the blade could retract with enough speed, a proof-of-concept video convinced us that the project was worth tackling. If someone gets to keep a finger thanks to the final product, then it will be worth the work.

The night took an interesting turn with the next winning concept, a board game that inventor Lynn Higgins has used to teach reading for over 16 years. We liked that Lynn’s concept was not only educational, but proven to be effective, and while a board game may seem like an odd choice for a Quirky product, we’re confident that our design staff and community can give this invention a 21st century update. Hooked on Phonics: eat your heart out.

After our little foray into education, we returned to the realm of “OMG WANT” with the stairway slide, invented by Trisha Cleveland. There’s not much nuance to this one folks: it’s an inflatable slide you deploy on your stairway, a classic case of “love it or hate it”. Luckily for Trisha (and children everywhere), the Quirky community loved it, with an unprecedented rating of 68%, and if there’s one thing we love, it’s giving the community what they want. Also… slides.

It’s no secret that we love prototypes here at Quirky, and Lester Quimpo proved to be an inventor after our own hearts, submitting an impressive concept for a baby bottle washer that he prototyped by hand. Some audience members questioned its similarity to a dishwasher, but several parents noted that dishwashers are not ubiquitous, and that many families go through bottles much faster than they run cycles. Moreover, we were impressed that Lester’s prototype was driven by hydraulics, without drawing electric power. In the end, after a heart debate, we decided to select the concept as an Explore, and have asked Lester to send us his prototype for further examination, and a discussion of future potential. Something tells us we won’t be disappointed.

The final invention pick of the night might seem a bit familiar: an app-enabled piggy bank by inventor Jason Hunter that was initially featured at our Wink Product Evaluation. While it didn’t make the cut on Wednesday, CEO Ben Kaufman believed that it was worth another look, and pitched it as an exciting update of a classic concept with limitless potential: integrated tech could transform it into full miniature bank, complete with burgulary alarms, account tracking, and features that teach kids to save and manage money. Ben’s avid pitch swayed both the crowd and community, and with this much enthusiasm right off the bat, we can’t wait to see how the finished product will turn out.
