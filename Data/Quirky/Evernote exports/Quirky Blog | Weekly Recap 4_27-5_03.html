Weekly Recap 4/27-5/03

posted 15 days ago by Nathaniel

Happy Friday everyone! Yet another week is behind us and the weekend approaches as a time to reflect on what transpired. It was a busy seven days, full of glowing press, project phases, and new blog posts to inspire the inventor in all of us. If you missed any of it we’re happy to get you up to speed with this week’s recap.

In the press world, the Huffington Post started off the week with a piece titled “What Is the Maker Movement and Why Should You Care?”, which mentioned Quirky as a startup that equips makers with access to resources that turn ideas into products. Mashable followed suit by dropping a quick mention of Quirky in their piece about how to land your dream job, and Modern Tokyo Times named Quirky as one of three innovative companies to watch in 2013. Real Simple included Vine in their “6 Clever Items to Simplify Your Life” roundup, and Slimline was reviewed by Poshmom. Lastly, Daily Record loved the innovation behind Broom Groomer and Pivot Power, Gizmodo reviewed the customer stamp maker, Pixit, and The Awesomer highlighted one of the newest Quirky products in development, Rotoblade.

The versatile Rotoblade made waves on The Awesomer

Product development projects began on Monday with CMF and Tagline phases for Kitchen Disks, and a CMF project for the Bluetooth Bracelet. On Tuesday we launched the iPad keyboard case, Flashcase, to the Quirky Upcoming page, as well as opened Research phases for both the Nursing Diaper Bag and Rice for One. Wednesday was the day we launched a CMF phase for the Mac Mini Flip Stand and brainstormed the Collapsible Bike Helmet, the Musical Train Tracks, and the Multipurpose Sensor. Thursday morning we introduced the double basin cake pan, Layer Up, to the Upcoming page, and opened community design projects for the Nursing Diaper Bag and the Smart Piggy Bank, a design phase for Rice for One, and a Tagline phase for the Bluetooth Bracelet. Finally, on Friday we unveiled the carpentry scribe tool, Trimline, on the Upcoming page, and opened a tool attachment research phase for Switch v2, a CMF project for PowerCurl v2, and our first Wink product research phase for the App Enabled Egg Tray.

Trimline is the newest tool in Quirky’s product portfolio

It was a slower than usual week on the blog on account of the masterful Mr. Baron von Fancy’s absence, but we still managed to push out a few new pieces for your enjoyment. Using the amazing power of the internet to write and schedule posts remotely, Baron started off the week with a new Monday Design Tip focused on the value of immersing one’s self in the subject one is designing for. That same day he gave us a sneak peak of Quirky Head of Community Steven Shaw’s take on the beloved Gin with… series, in which he officially introduced the Quirky community to Invention Ambassador, Anna Buchbauer. On Tuesday you were given a glimpse of Friday’s new naming game-focused Feedback Friday, and on Thursday we introduced you to Quirky’s new Resident Storyteller, Stephen Robert Morse. Finally, we wrapped up the week as we always do with the Invention Picks of the Week.

So there you have it: another full week at QHQ summed up in three concise paragraphs. Make sure to check back with us next week for another seven days of invention made accessible. Have a great weekend and enjoy these recordings of Low Calorie Soft Drink with… Anna Buchbauer and our naming game themed Freedback Friday.
