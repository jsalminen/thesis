Invention Picks of the Week!

posted 2 days ago by Invention Ambassadors


Members of the US Wrestling team and Girl Scout Troop 3304 joined in on last night’s EVAL as Baron Nagy lead the way. Our guests Stacy, Product Manager at Percolate Industries, and Katie, 8 year old Girl Scout, did an incredible job as panelists. Over the course of an hour and fifteen minutes, 12 of the week’s best inventions were discussed and debated, with four ultimately being chosen for Quirky’s collaborative development process. If you missed the party, a full recording of the event can be viewed at the end of this post, but for those on a schedule, here’s a quick run-through of our newest Quirky inventors:

The first invention of the night came from Orce Vasilev, who pitched a kit for creating the perfect 3D cookies.  The kit divides into two parts, reflecting the shape of an “animal” that then can be merged to make one great 3D cookie. Although some concerns were raised about the viability of the product, ultimately the crowd and the community thought it was way too cool of an idea to pass up.  If the Girl Scout loved this idea, it would be foolish for Quirky not to take it on! (Video Segment)

The second pick was submitted by inventor InDezign, who wanted to take away the pain in wrapping gifts by creating a very unique wrapping paper holder and cutter. It features an open cylinder that allows the wrapping paper roll to conveniently slide inside of it. Also, it features a retractable blade that makes a straight cut down the paper.  The live community voting meter raised all the way to 78% which Baron indicated was a “biblically high number” and was definitely a winning idea in the minds of the community. (Video Segment)

Jeroen Inventions joined the Quirky Community early in 2013 and we’re happy to crown his first invention idea. Using an aperture design, the Adjustable Pan Cover expands and contracts with a simple twist. This all-in-one lid occupies less kitchen cupboard space when not in use and it fits perfectly on any size pot or pan on the cooktop. Whether you’re adding this product to your existing collection of lids or you’re investing in one cover for your assortment of pots and pans, the cook in your home will appreciate this versatile piece. (Video Segment)


Harry Clarke sent us a beautiful sample of his Flat Fold Tongs that made their debut at Live Eval. As a woodworker by trade, he took advantage of the natural flexibility of wood to create a spring action in his kitchen tong. For storage, the same tongs fold backwards into a slim, compact form. Although the concept of folding tongs isn’t brand new, we saw invention in the sleek construction and crowned Harry a brand new Quirky Inventor. We’re excited to experiment with different materials in the design process and bring this tool to life. (Video Segment)
