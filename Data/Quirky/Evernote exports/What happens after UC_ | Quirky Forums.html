David Hopkins | 09/26/2012 | 04:23 PM

Ok so to keep with the string of "what happens after UC" - now my dartboard has officially been in UC for 3 weeks and 1 day. I've emailed my invention ambassador after getting the phone call last week on Tuesday that they were still working on it with no response. Once on the day of the call and once yesterday. No response. I just go straight to voicemail when I call.
.
I have been a business professional for over 15 years and I'm not used to this level of "lack of communication". I honestly don't even need a big long email, just a quick blurb stating "we are still working on it" or something to that effect.
.
From their email to me on 9/10/12 - a full week after it went UC - "I also wanted to take a moment and give you a heads up about what it means to be Under Consideration. Everyday, the Quirky staff goes through all of the ideas submitted to our website. Everyone here is responsible for finding the very best ideas with the most potential. When someone finds an idea that they want to share with the rest of the staff, they pull it out of the pool of submissions and put it under UC, so that it can be presented to the Quirky staff for review.
.
One of two things will happen after your idea is reviewed; either your idea will be chosen and we will move to EVAL (We’ll call and let you know), or your idea will not move forward and we’ll reach out to you by leaving our reasons for not choosing your idea in your feedback section of your page (underneath your submission and above your idea’s comment section) with some additional information about resubmitting ideas and some ways to improve your ideation."
.
I'm of course hoping that the latter doesn't happen but 2 full weeks of anxiously awaiting a phone call and continuously checking my ideation page (underneath your submission and above your idea’s comment section) is getting a bit nerve-wracking.
.
And of course I'm praying that my inability for patience doesn't hurt my chance either. I've been working on this for my entire life and to be so close to "hurry up and wait" is killing me.
