Skin Sensing Utility Knife knows the instant it touches skin and retracts the blade to safety. Proof of concept video included.

Electronics
Submitted by: Neil MacAloney
.

The Problem

Utility knives are an incredibly useful tool for cutting a multitude of materials. However, they are very dangerous, especially in the hands of less-skilled users and amateur craftsman. I've had dozens of near misses that could have turned out very badly.

The Solution

Utility knives have not evolved much over the years, but now the technology exists for utility knives to sense when they are in contact with skin and retract the blade safely without injuring the user. By insulating the blade from the handle and having one electrical contact to the blade, a small microprocessor can utilize capacitance sensing to continuously monitor the status of the blade and know the instant it comes in contact with skin. This is demonstrated in the attached video I made with a blade and an Arduino microprocessor. Using the skin sensor as a trigger, the mechanism that locks the blade down could be released by a fast acting solenoid to retract the blade fully and prevent countless injuries. This type of skin sensing would work for cutting most materials except in certain circumstances where conductive materials are in contact with another portion of the user's body. For this, an override switch would be necessary. The vast majority of materials cut with utility knives are not conductive and would work with this system. The unit could be powered by a 9V battery with an LED indicating the operational status of the knife. An additional blinking red LED would indicate that the override button is being depressed and the skin sensor is not active. The blade would rotate up and away from the sharp edge so that it creates space between skin and the blade during retraction.

Key Features

  • This relatively low-tech skin sensing circuit could prevent users from accidently cutting themselves.
  • Status LEDs indicate operational and override methods of operation.
