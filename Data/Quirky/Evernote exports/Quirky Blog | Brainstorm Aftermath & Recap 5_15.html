Brainstorm Aftermath & Recap 5/15

posted 3 days ago by anna

We had good, clean Brainstorm fun last night with three easy to love ideas. All ideas would be great for kids, and brought out the Quirky kids at heart (meet them here). The highlight of the night was mangling a few watermelons in the name of invention (don’t worry…none were seriously injured or wasted. We quickly devoured the messy remains). Not to worry if you missed out on all the excitement, get up to speed right…now!

Product 613: Watermelon Pop Maker

Inventor Timothy Houle (also Inventor of our beloved Stem) had a stroke of inspiration for a tool to make watermelon popsicles while carving his Jack-o-Lantern for Halloween. Tim envisions a product similar to an apple corer, but with a much larger cylinder and able to grip and hold the rind to keep the pop together. As our fabulous intern Bernardo demonstrated, there’s some messy work to be done to achieve the perfect watermelon popsicle.

Product 631: Adjustable Pen

While watching his daughter create elaborate greeting cards with multiple pens, Inventor Harmeet Singh thought up the idea for a single pen with adjustable widths. Last night, our designers began imagining how they too might benefit from using this highly convenient pen. We even spotted our little invention on Core77′s blog, asking its design-centered audience their thoughts about it. Could this Brainstorm be the start of the pen revolution?

Product 592: Doggie Plinko Game

It’s hard not to love  Marc Rumaner‘s dog puzzle based on The Price is Right’s Plinko game. We deliberated many pet game related issues, including dog size, education, owner interaction, and frequency of treats. The community dreamed big on this one, leading us to thoughts of expanding doggie plinko for cats, squirrels, and ….turtles? Watch the hilarity unfold here.
