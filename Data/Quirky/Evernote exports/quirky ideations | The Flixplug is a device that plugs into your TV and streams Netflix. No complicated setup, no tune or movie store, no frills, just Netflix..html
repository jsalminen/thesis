The Flixplug is a device that plugs into your TV and streams Netflix. No complicated setup, no tune or movie store, no frills, just Netflix.

Electronics
Submitted by: Emmanuel Saadia
 

The Problem

A friend asked me for the cheapest way to stream Netflix on his flat screen TV which would not involve

1) connecting his computer to the TV;

2) buying a $100+ device such as an AppleTV or a Roku player or whatnot;

3) could be operated by his kids;

His problem is common, there are more than 20M netflix subscribers in the US, and several millions abroad (including Canada, UK and Ireland).

 

The Solution

Introducing the FLIXPLUG: a tiny android-based computer, no bigger than a plug. Such device can currently be bought for around $50 (see image above). It does output HD video through HDMI, and connects to the internet either through wifi or ethernet. However, the software and user experience are that of a mobile phone without a keyboard and mouse, on a TV screen! That is, as such it is completely useless as a consumer product.

The flixplug can be manufactured for much cheaper than the competition, and should be packaged and marketed as a single-use device instead of a full-fledged computer. Its version of Android will be customized to accomodate a single type of usage (streaming movies and tv shows from Netflix and/or other subscription services such as Amazon Instant Video or Hulu, Flixster etc...). It will be controlled remotely by a phone app and/or a computer app, using the home wifi network to communicate.

 

Key Features

  • The Flixplug does only one thing and does it extremely well - stream movies and tv shows from online subscription services directly onto your tv;
  • The Flixplug leverages the ease of use of open-source mobile computing to deliver PC-quality high-definition video in an affordable package;
  • Already exists (thus considerably reducing time to market), but geared for tech enthusiasts, not consumers.

Product Comparison

  • AppleTV
  • Roku
  • Boxee Box
  • Any so-called "smart tv" or "connected tv" that comes with apps in standard
  • Any GoogleTV device
