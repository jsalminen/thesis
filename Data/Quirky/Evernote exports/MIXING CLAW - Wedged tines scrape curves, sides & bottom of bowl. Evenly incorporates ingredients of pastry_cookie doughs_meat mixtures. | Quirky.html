MIXING CLAW - Wedged tines scrape curves, sides & bottom of bowl. Evenly incorporates ingredients of pastry/cookie doughs/meat mixtures.

Kitchen
Submitted by: CKLeming
.

The Problem

When I am mixing heavy cookie doughs, (like a double-batch of my favorite oatmeal chocolate chip with nuts) I like to use a large serving fork for mixing, so it will meld & mesh the ingredients evenly throughout the batter. I also like using my large fork, because I can drag dough from the bottom of the bowl & fold it into the mixture easily.

My problem is that when using the large fork for mixing, there is always extra flour caked on the bottom & in the curves of my mixing bowl, so I have to grab a spatula to try to get that incorporated into my dough, as well. Likewise, when I am making meat mixtures (like meatloaf), there are always stray bits of diced onion, egg & seasonings that never quite make it into the mixture, when I use a wooden spoon.

The Solution

My solution is the Mixing Claw. The curved wedge-shaped tines act as small spatulas & will scrape the sides & curved edges of your mixing bowl. It will also drag & lift heavy cookie dough from the bottom of the bowl to the top of the mixture to make sure that all ingredients are incorporated thoroughly.

Key Features

  • Wedge-shaped tines that can scrape the sides & bottom of your mixing bowl to better incorporate ingredients
  • Curved claw design, to lift & drag heavy dough & meat mixtures from the bottom of the bowl to facilitate the folding action
