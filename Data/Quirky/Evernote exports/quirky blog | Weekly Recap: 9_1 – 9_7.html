Weekly Recap: 9/1 – 9/7

posted 1 day ago by Nathaniel

September sure has started with a bang. We realize that most weeks are pretty crazy in the Quirky-verse, but this one was particularly jam-packed.

The bulk of this week’s press concerned last night’s funding announcement. Ben shared his perspective on fundraising in an insightful blog post that everyone should read. The story also ran in the Wall Street Journal (who also made a video),  Business Insider (twice), Pando Daily, Techonomy.com, AllthingsD.com, Yahoo News, Venture Beat, GigaOm, Techcrunch, Wired, The New York Times, Inc, Fortune, Xconomy.com, Beta Beat, Silicon Valley Business Journal , and Upstart Business Journal. Phew. We’re glad that’s over. Now it’s time to get back to work and use that cash to make invention even more accessible.

In addition to all the hubub surrounding yesterday’s announcement, Quirky products made several appearances throughout the week. Design World’s 8/29 interview with Quirky’s Head of Engineering, Research, Development, and Profoundness, John Jacobsen, continued to attract readers throughout the week for it’s focus on small manufacturing and 3D printing. Real Simple magazine included Totem in it’s August issue and on its website, and Pen Zen made an appearance on one of my favorite sites for finding cool stuff, TheAwesomer.com. FoxNews.com’s Back to School round up included Bobble Brush as one of the recommended gadgets and MilkMaid was listed in a story on futuristic gadgets from the Huffington Post. Finally, Vancouver.com included Pivot Power in a story about dorm room accessories.

On the product development side of things, we launched eight new projects this week! First, Product Naming was opened for Stealth Shelves, followed by Community Design phases for the Garment Stapler, the Emergency Sign, the Shoe Lace Tool, the Quick Release Bike Fender, and the Power Strip with Portable Power. Additionally, the Multipurpose Grapnel had a Refine phase this week, Product Research commenced for the Quick Release Bike Fender, and we announced and launched a new Ethnographic research phase for Pod Power. As far a product launches are concerned, we introduced Mesa and Enfold to the Upcoming page, and announced that we’ll be making Squeak! We also brainstormed the Bicycle Brake Signal and the Jelly Bracelet Maker.

If you follow this blog regularly you know that we published content to help you craft great submissions and keep you in the loop on Cordies Executive’s progress. Additionally, we posted updates on the Simple Solutions promotion we are running with Target. While the submission period ended on Tuesday, there is still a lot of work to do in order for us to pick the top ideas. For those of you who are curious about what is to be done about your #spotsv ideas that are still live, we provided an update on that too. Finally, if you saw Chad Hetherington’s recent tweets you’ll know that Quirky products are now being sold in Best Buy!

And that concludes yet another week’s recap. If you think we missed anything, please email us at questions@quirky.com or leave a comment below.
