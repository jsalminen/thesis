Unofficial Guide to the Threadless Forums

This guide was created to instruct new members on basic forum etiquette and address the most frequently asked questions on the Threadless forums. With many new members coming to the forums daily, the same questions and issues arise, hence this guide.


This guide is reflective of the Threadless forum community, but is by no means definitive. Disregarding these guidelines will not get you banned or deleted. This guide is simply meant to instruct beginners, address their concerns, and enable them to become contributing members of the community with a minimum of hassle and irritation for all parties involved.


For any design and submission related questions, first consult the Make section on Threadless before posting to the forums.


Basic Forum Etiquette

1. Use the official Threadless Promotion thread to promote your designs.
Do not create a new thread in the 'General' forum for the express purpose of promoting your design and soliciting votes. Utilize other social media sites to promote your designs.


2. Do not hijack another member’s thread or submission page by soliciting votes.


3. Ask for feedback BEFORE submitting your design.
Even better, create a work-in-progress thread to solicit feedback from the community. Once the design is up for voting on Threadless, it’s more than fine to turn it into a promotion thread.


4. Do not post the same topic in multiple forums or the same forum.
Doing this will not increase your exposure. It will only annoy people. The forum categories are loosely followed at best. If your question is reasonable, be patient; someone will answer.


5. Do not create a thread asking why your design was rejected.
This is one of the most frequently asked questions on the Threadless forums. When a submission is rejected, the Threadless staff provides a brief explanation. Most times the reasons are self-evident.


If the reasons are still unclear, by all means, ask the forums. However, do not simply post to complain or express indignation. Instead, ask for feedback on how to improve your design. The community will be more than happy to oblige.


Frequently Asked Questions

1. How does voting work on Threadless?
Designs go up for voting on Threadless for a period of seven days. This period can be longer for sponsored challenges, but the minimum is seven days. At the end of seven days, your design will receive an average score for the votes received.


THIS IS THE IMPORTANT BIT. Your score and comments do not automatically determine if your design will be printed. They serve as a guide for the Threadless staff to select what designs are printed. In the case of sponsored challenges, the sponsoring company makes the final decision.


Do not complain that the process is unfair. Threadless as a company has the right to select the designs it prints. It does not exist to appease your ego.


2. How long does it take for designs to be selected for print?


The standard answer is that it can take up to 90 days for a design to be selected for print. However, designs can be selected for print any time in that 90-day period after voting ends. After that period, if you haven't received word from Threadless, you can assume your design is rejected.


3. Can I re-submit a design that has been rejected?


Rejected designs may be resubmitted after the initial 90 days provided that substantial changes are made. The same design without any significant changes will not be accepted for submission again.



The rationale for this guide is that there are many new members who jump on the forums to promote their designs, never to be heard from again. There are also many self-interested types looking to make quick money on Threadless and spamming the forums with promotion threads.


There is a thriving community on the Threadless forums. If you invest the time, not only will you grow creatively, you’ll ensure that Threadless continues to be the best community-driven t-shirt site period.


This guide will be updated as needed. Please help keep this thread near the top of the forums for the sake of all our sanity.
