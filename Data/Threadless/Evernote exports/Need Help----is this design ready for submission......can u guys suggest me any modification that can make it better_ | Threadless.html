I like it. I would try and transition the edges of the shadowy area a little (most notably bottom left), and either burn the lines to strengthen them or trace them in a separate layer with a hard black brush too, either that or change the black and white threshold to make it jump out a little more. I guess what I'm saying is that the pencil style drawing and the large bright red area don't really gel together like this, you'll need to strengthen one or weaken the other. It looks good other than that, but I won't claim to get the concept behind it. Good luck with it.

I don't think the concept is strong enough to do well here but if you want to finish it for practise, I would work on the colours and try to make clean lineart instead of the sketchy style. Maybe also experiment with a background - sand texture or tiles, or something else to represent where the shoe is.

Mind It said:

Here a shoe is trying to tell everyone that stop walking/coming towards me....... Yes I want to make it better. Should I use more colors in it?

Would you buy that shirt? I mean if it was a perfect picture without the weird shadow and colour strength mismatches? If your answer is no, then give it up. If you are completely committed to making this work then you will need to make it technically a lot better. Their are various tutorials to help you out there on-line. In regards to this, I would say at the least you need to cut the image away from that shadow using free select/lasso tool, and my previous comment about the colour strength stands. I learned the hard way to listen to these people, they know their stuff, trust me. Ask yourself if it is a strong enough concept to warrant the work it will need on the art, if not ditch it and move on.
