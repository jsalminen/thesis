I, too, am not a copyright expert, but this is something I'd put in the category of "appropriation". Because the idea of the design is to imagine a Daft Punk poster in the Star Wars world, it only "works" if the composition of the poster is the same as an easily recognizable Daft Punk image. It's like trying to incorporating the Mona Lisa into a design; you can't NOT copy the Mona Lisa because the whole point is that it IS the Mona Lisa. So I'd say this one is OK.

That being said, copying a photo because you don't know how to draw something is NOT the same thing and is plagiarism. I've had someone who copied a photo of an eagle start off by saying that they used the photo for reference because they weren't able to take a photo themselves and couldn't draw it from their head, and then later claim it was used as appropriation. Using photos for reference and appropriating them are completely different conceptually; you can't claim both.
