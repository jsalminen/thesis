The Electronic Wasteland

The American news broadcast 60 Minutes did a story on electronic waste which is worth watching.
you-can-learn-more-about-this-broadcast-here-http-cbsn.ws-84hy
If you're curious to see what can happen to e-waste that is improperly discarded or treated, this is a good place to start. I think it's important to note that this isn't always what happens, but it's eye-opening to see the reality of where some of our electronics can end up.

I think the message that's particularly important is that it's tough for consumers, and sometimes even for recycling companies, to always trust that they know where their e-waste goes. 

How might we create, for instance, user-verified mapping tools that help consumers track where their e-waste is going? Or what about rating systems to provide consumers with verification that the recycler they're working with is legitimate?

How might we help consumers understand their options when their products have reached the end of their useful life?
