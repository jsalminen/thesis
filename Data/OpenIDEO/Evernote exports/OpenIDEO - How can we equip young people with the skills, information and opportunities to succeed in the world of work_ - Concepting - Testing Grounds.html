Testing Grounds

Have young secondary school students work on weekly projects covering a range of job types.
http://rogeriopereira.blogspot.com/2010_04_01_archive.html
Students, age 13-15, working in groups of three or four would work on weekly projects. Each week the project would focus on a new area, from diagnosing a patient, to creating a business model, to designing a better desk. The idea would be to get a broad set of topics for the students to sample. Each week the teams would be matched with an expert in the field to help guide them and provide suggestions or resources.Â 

Each week would begin with a trip to the workplace for the related topic. This is to provide context to the work and help the students visualize working there in the future. The following three days would be dedicated to working on the project. This would probably happen at some other dedicated location. Finally, the week would end with a reflection.

The students would be required to document their experiences. This way at the end of each project, the students could reflect and evaluate their own perspective on the work they did. At the end of the entire workshop students could compare their previous evaluations to see which they were most interested in. After a time of self-reflection, students would meet with councilors to discuss what they've discovered and how they might use this information to design their future.Â 


Weekly schedule:
Day 1 - visit to a local office relevant to the weekly project.
Days 2,3,4 - assign and work on the project
Day 5 - recap and evaluate

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

It will enable them to understand their passions earlier on and create goals to help them be fulfilled.
It offers a structured opportunity to explore, while making new connections with peers as well as professionals they may one day work with.
It would provide group work experience

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

I believe the reflection and evaluation would help most. Built on the experiences of working on a broad range of topics the students would be able to make a much more informed decision about their personal interests and passions and how they would like to include them in their lives.
