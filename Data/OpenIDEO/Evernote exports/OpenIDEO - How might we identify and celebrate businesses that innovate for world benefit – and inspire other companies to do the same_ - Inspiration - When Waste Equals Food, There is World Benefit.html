When Waste Equals Food, There is World Benefit

Waste equals food is one of the most fundamental design principles in nature. The insulation and packaging are composted after their useful lives and in this way become food for the natural system. This company is making money changing packaging.
picture of compostable packaging, courtesy of ecovativedesign.com/

Waste equals food is one of the most fundamental design principles in nature.About five years ago I had the good fortune to meet two young engineers, Eben Bayer and Gavin McIntyre, who had taken this principle to heart.They were in the early launch stages of a company called Ecovative Design.Their intention was to grow insulation for houses and packaging materials from a mix of fungus and grain husks.The insulation and packaging would be composted after their useful lives and in this way become food for the natural system.

They had several challenges at the time but two major ones were: reducing the time it took to grow the products from thirteen days to five days or less and making sure that the fungus spores were killed before they came into contact with users.Based on a recent article in USA Today, Ecovative Design personnel have solved these problems.Today Ecovative Design has a strong and growing customer base because they have found a cost effective and truly environmentally friendly solution for packaging materials that many industries and their customers are demanding. They are suppliers to computer manufacturers like HP, household products companies like Crate and Barrel, and auto industry bumper manufacturers.

We all benefit when we can remove plastic packaging waste from our landfills and act to restore our depleted topsoil.These two young men have successfully looked to Nature for a solution and are now pioneering a new industry.I am confident that their company will grow just as I am confident that their approach will inspire others to search out world benefit approaches in their industries.
