Micro-Recycling

Small but efficient recycling plants developed close to sources of e-waste and close to bespoke manufacturing.
 
Scale down diagram
At the moment the economics of recycling require large, centralised plants.

However if efforts are put into down-sizing processes (perhaps starting with local pre-processing),  incorporating as much re-use as possible but gradually incorporating emerging technologies like microbial extraction.

As was pointed out in an inspiration (link will be included on update) the new product is manufactured centrally but widely distributed in use.



There is an opportunity for combining micro-recycling with micro-manufacture which (hopefully) can change the economic viability of smaller scale recycling.



Resources extracted by 'mining' e-waste would obviously be fed back into the manufacturing chain but if this too is down-scaled there are opportunities for smaller business, local employment and value adding. In addition this reduces transport costs, localizes the resources produced and stops the drain of those resources back overseas.

 
 

How does your concept safeguard human health and protect our environment?

With appropriate safeguards incorporated in such micro-recycling plants there should be no increase in risk. Obviously any process that takes waste out of the landfill stream is of both health and environmental benefit.
 

Where does your concept fit into the lifecycle of electronic devices?

The concept alters the focus of recycling to supporting a burgeoning micro-manufacturing industry rather than centralised bulk processing.
 

What steps could be taken today to start implementing your concept?

Initial research into how recycled materials can be incorporated into small manufacturing processes.
 

What kinds of resources will be needed to fully implement and scale your concept?

It would require a change in thinking from some current organisations, possibly along the lines of Nucor (micro-furnacing) and Blue Oak Resources
