Gaming for E-Waste Behavior Change

What if we could make the process of learning about, raising awareness of, and actively recycling discarded electronics into a game that everyone could play?
post-its on gaming for e-waste

Today at IDEO Palo Alto we hosted an E-Waste Challenge brainstorm (using the Brainstorm in a Box toolkit).

'Gamification' is a topic we've seen come up in our Inspiration phase – what might this game system actually look like? How would it work if we applied gaming to e-waste?

What if a person could earn points for collecting unused/old/discarded electronics and turning them into a e-waste recycler? For instance, turning in a phone charger, a phone and a toaster could get you 10 points. What might you be able to trade those points in for? What organization might help you redeem them?

Similarly, we brainstormed the idea of using levels to encourage tiered actions. One thing we got excited about was using levels to get people to involve other individuals and networks in e-waste recycling. Maybe some people take on roles in the game and earn badges, eg: people who are particularly good at leveraging their online networks and creating social media e-waste campaigns might earn the 'Connector' badge.

We also talked about using Facebook or another platform to log a person's electronics purchase and recycling habits – might seeing how your recycling habits rate against your friends' inspire you to recycle more?

We even talked about negative incentives or penalties to demotivate people from buying new.

What elements of gaming do you think would work best for e-waste?
