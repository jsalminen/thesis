Eye Care and Microfranchise With VisionSpring

VisionSpring uses a microfranchising model to deliver affordable eyeglasses to BoP communities around the world.
VisionSpring Global Video
VisionSpring trains local entrepreneurs in eye care and business management, then supplies each with a 'Business in a Bag' sales kit to launch a microfranchise. Entrepreneurs then work with organizations such as schools and churches to organize vision screenings, to refer individuals to optometrists as needed, and ultimately to sell affordable eyeglasses to the community. 

Aside: VisionSpring is the main partner for Warby Parker's "buy a pair, give a pair" model. WP doesn't distribute its own eyewear directly to the target individuals; rather, they provide funding to VS to reach and train more low-income entrepreneurs.

Hm, you're right—VS is a non-profit. Still, in this inspiration phase, there may be value in considering what they are or are not doing right, as it looks like they're trying to refine their model in hopes of making it self-sustaining. The 'Business In a Bag' kits and eyeglasses aren't given away for free—they are sold—which helps VS recover some of its costs. The idea, then, is to train local entrepreneurs to be "microfranchises" so that over time, VS can rely less and less on donations and more on portions of sales revenue from the microfranchises.
