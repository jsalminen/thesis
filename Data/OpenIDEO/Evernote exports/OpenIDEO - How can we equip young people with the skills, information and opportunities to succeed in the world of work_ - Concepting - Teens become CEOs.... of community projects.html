Teens become CEOs.... of community projects

Online platform that allows teens to identify, create, staff, and implement team-based projects that benefit their local communities.
Example
  • Megan, a 17-year-old living in Barrow, England, realizes that her town desperately needs a small skate park.  All of the current options are illegal and/or unsafe.
  • Megan uses the online platform to create a new project, "Skate Park for Barrow," and becomes CEO of the project.
  • Using the social media tools integrated into the platform, Megan shares the project with her friends.
  • Megan defines the positions she is looking to fill, and begins to staff the team.  Four of her friends form her Board of Directors to help oversee the project, another becomes the CFO in charge of fundraising and budgeting, another becomes Chief Architect, another the Head of Construction...
  • As the project progresses, the platform allows Megan and her team to discuss ideas, create a timeline, create a public project website, track fundraising, share pictures and other new developments amongst their wider network, etc.
  • After several months of planning, revising, and fundraising, the project culminates with Megan, her team, a group of local volunteers she recruited on Facebook, and some hired contractors building the skate park over the course of several days.
  • Upon successful completion, the project's public website remains up as a permanent, positive web footprint for all of the teens involved.

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

Allows teens to develop both hard and soft business skills on a "micro" level, alongside their friends, working towards a goal that is a meaningful to them. The projects would have tangible results that would be easy for others (read: future employers!) to understand and appreciate.

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

Online platform would include simple versions of the tools mentioned above (staffing, project coordination, scheduling, fundraising, public website templates, etc.) and the ability to share everything across existing social networking sites.

The expectation is that the culmination of each project would be the in-person work itself. This might be a one day event for a small group of friends (removing graffiti from a certain block, for example) or a longer-term project (like the skate park) requiring many people and some degree of outsourced work.
