Startup Scramble

There's a rising tide of entrepreneurism around the world at the moment, with people deciding to grow the jobs & companies which must exist to create the change we want to see in the world. Breed entrepreneurial spirit for World Changing Business!
 
Change The World - thanks to Mashable Social Good
"Start A Business, Change The World"


The landscape of economics, work and employment are dry and dull to many young people.  We often forget to fill in the detail of WHY we expect young people to get a job, instead focusing on the HOW & the WHAT.


Re-engaging young people with that why is very important, and there's a rising tide of challenges which face us socially, environmentally, culturally & economically. If we can connect young people with the challenges they care passionately about, and give them the skills to do something about it by themselves or collectively, we can equip them with the knowledge about how to be an active global citizen and the experience of being a positive force for change.  That's more important than contributing to the economy.
 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

Most of us learn more through experience than we do in a classroom / theoretical situation, so this idea focuses on participative, experiential learning, conversation & action.



Startup Scramble has been used as a term in the past to denote entrepreneurial activities at colleges in the US. I would like to see the format take several streams into account:



CONNECT - get in touch with what it is that you care about, why you care about it, and better understand the complexity of the problem. This is a mix of the early stages of IDEO's own Human Centred Design process, and Collaboration Cafe (developed in Wellington).



CO-CREATE - it's powerful to work on ideas in small groups, so use the 'cafe' style groupings to start proposing interventions to the need that had been identified in the earlier steps. Work together in high-intensity environments like Good For Nothing (goodfornothing.co) to take a leap forward in 48 hours.



DO - now the idea is up & running with some core assets, it's time to learn & experience by doing. Learning to be an entrepreneur is often a difficult set of skills to master, but by using learning frameworks like Lean Startup, can help to give a process as a 'guide rope' to better solutions, whilst encouraging the participants to innovate on top of this framework to become more autonomous.



COLLABORATE - a key facet to tackling complex problems is collaboration, so encouraging the young people to work in collaborative environments such as co-working spaces with facilitated gatherings (http://socialinnovation.ca/ for example) is important to continually encouraging cross-disciplinary thinking & collaboration.



The aim with this program is to encourage young people to create their own world of work, not simply adhere to the status quo reality. If we all simply transitioned into the world of work, we would have no Google, no Facebook, no Solar Energy, and very low levels of innovation in the world. We need flexible, creative, collaborative people, not worker drones - connecting people with true motivation is an important part of creating this future; purpose, autonomy, mastery & being outside of our comfort zones are the key elements to motivation. Not money.

 

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

Some elements of this idea could be delivered as online platforms, but experiential learning is much better delivered in person, in rich holistic human experiences.

Absolutely - yes thanks for posting Dan Pink & Simon Simenek - two very influential TED talks for me, and I should add we're working with a lot of Dan Pink's ideas over at Enspiral (http://www.enspiral.com) .



How would it all hang together? Well I would view it as a program which is based over a 6-12 month period, along the same lines as School for Social Entrepreneurs, with some more holistic discussion, action weekends & a permanent space. Simple really.



Sasha's LAB21 idea looks great - very much like the co-working spaces which are spreading around the world, except for younger people. We have one here in Wellington, but The Hub is probably best known around the world for it's work, and I'm a big fan of what Centre for Social Innovation have done in Toronto: http://socialinnovation.ca/



Youth Cafe looks good - definitely coming from the same idea space as World Cafe, Open Space, and the next evolution we're developing in Wellington - Collaboration Cafe. I'm a big fan of small group, conversive experiences which are lightly facilitated for co-creation & innovation.



I like Andrew's idea for Teens becoming CEO's, but would add some more offline elements to support the online experience.
