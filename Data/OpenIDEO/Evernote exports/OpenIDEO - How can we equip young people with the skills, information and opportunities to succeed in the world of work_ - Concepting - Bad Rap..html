Bad Rap.

Use music, with storytelling and humor, to teach professional soft skills. In this initiative, record labels invite street kids, store managers, and professionals to have major artists re-tell their stories through music.
Banksy street art: Making the street culture holy. Courtesy of streetartutopia.com
Record labels could co-sponsor a competition in which people upload stories or video clips of themselves telling a story in response to question prompts such as:
  • Share a story: What mistake did you make to learn a rule of the road in business?
  • Explain your needs: What mistakes do you see your new hires make all the time?
  • Share the pain: How does it feel to lose an employee?
  • Share the pain: How does it feel to lose a job?
  • Share a story: How did you prove yourself?Â 
  • Share a story: How did your work make a difference in someone's life?
These real-life stories and videos could serve as an archive of inspiration for artists to make music that will spread knowledge and make an impact on the world. People's songs will be dedicated to them -- or their town if that is the individual's preference. Record companies could even sponsor compilation albums of with each of their artists each telling someone's story.

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

The music stories should capture and spread a basic social awareness of what the working world is like --- in good ways and in bad --- so that young people can recognize different business situations and have heard examples of appropriate responses.

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

- Video kits for retailers' regional managers to ask the assistant managers and mangers who have been promoted through the ranks to participate in story telling.
- Pop-up booths to capture stories of the unemployed and the employed.
- A website where people can easily activate a webcam to record and upload their story --- possibly with participation from local libraries which have computers with microphones and webcams
