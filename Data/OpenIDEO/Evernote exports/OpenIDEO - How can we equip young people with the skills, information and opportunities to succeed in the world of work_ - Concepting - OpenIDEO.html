OpenIDEO

Encourage young people to learn and develop skills by contributing to OpenIDEO
 
OpenIDEO

Challenges have been tackled successfully at OpenIDEO. These challenges require different skills to solve and so there is little doubt that by participating and contributing at OpenIDEO young people can learn and develop several skills that will aid them in the world of work.  By Studying past OpenIDeator Insights and Professor Anne-Laure's  relections on being an OpenIDEATOR (https://openideo.zendesk.com/entries/20794973-reflections-being-an-openideator) one can easily conclude that young people learn a lot when they contribute at OpenIDEO

 

What I am proposing here is that OpenIDEO is itself an amazing platform for young people to learn and develop skills and therefore, OpenIDEO can be considered as a practical solution to this challenge. Hence, it must promote itself exclusively to young people to encourage them to contribute and learn.

 

OpenIDEO can make a couple of additions to the site to encourage and help young people to contribute

1.  5 Video Tutorials demonstrating each aspect of the design thinking process and how to contribute to each part of the challenge. For example. How to Protoype ideas?

 

2. Complementing these Video tutorials, a "OpenIDEO DIY" kit that anyone can study and start contributing immediately. Similar to IDEO’s HCD kit.

 

OpenIDEO can take the following steps to promote itself as a platform where Young people can learn and develop Skills

1. Create a Short Documentary demonstrating how OpenIDEATORs have learned by contributing to challenges.

2. Extensively promote Open IDEO at Universities and Schools to increase awareness about OpenIDEO amongst young people.



 



 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

Young people will improve their creativity and problem solving skills



It will enhance their ability to research and explore; and draw insights from their observations.



It will help them become an integrative thinker.



They will have the opportunity to create something and work in a cross-functional global team or community.



Contributing will help them develop visualization and prototyping skills.



It will help them learn not only to how develop IDEAS but a articulate them.



They will learn how to build or enhance their IDEAs when they receive feedback by other members



The above will help them develop design-thinking skills, which will surely help them in the world of work.



As contributing involves collaboration, members will become better at collaborating (that too globally!).



It will allow them to build a network through collaboration.



They can be mentored by Seasoned OpenIDEO users as well as other experts on OpenIDEO.



In addition to the above Contributing at OpenIDEO can also help young people develop number of Soft Skills such as team work, social intelligence; self-confidence ;overcoming the fear of failure and community spirit



All these aspects will certainly enhance a member's job prospects and help them succeed in their work. Also, the fact that they have contributed to social challenge and are rewarded for it will boost their confidence and morale as they venture into the world of work.



 

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

As OpenIDEO is an online platform, the concept is online based.
