Guiding Principles for Our E-Waste Challenge



Our E-Waste Challenge tackles an important issue – one that we believe offers a lot of opportunity for impact-focused solutions. Still, the topic may feel overwhelming and complex to many of us learning about it for the first time. With that in mind we thought we'd take a minute to outline some Guiding Principles for our conversation and our efforts together.


Our Conversation

As always, the kind of conversation we have together sets the tone for the kind of ideas we'll design together. Here are a couple of things to keep in mind:

Focus on What Works

With a global issue like e-waste, it may seem easier at times to point fingers, find fault or lay blame. Rather than drudging up stories or specific country examples to showcase what's not working, let's focus on the success stories that we can learn from and be inspired by. What NGOs, organisations, businesses or governments are achieving results in this area? What stories of success can we highlight?

Look Forward, Not Backward

Again, let's not emphasise what's happened in the past, but instead reframe our conversation to focus on where we're heading. How can we inspire one another and collaborate to create solutions that drive our progress forward and in positive ways?

Stay Optimistic, Positive and Respectful

Our global community comprises an eclectic mix of experiences, opinions, perspectives and approaches – and there's real value in this diversity. Still, let's remember to keep our tone positive, our outlook optimistic and our dialogue respectful.


Our Focus

As we prepare for Concepting, we'll share more tips and tricks for designing successful concepts. For now, we wanted to offer a couple of focus areas for you to think about:

Environmental Stewardship and Human Health

For our concepts to be successful in this challenge, we'll need to be sure that the environment and people are equal parts of the equation. This means that while we're sourcing inspirations, let's play close attention to the success stories that show us how to manage e-waste in ways that protect our environment and support human health.

Consumers and Users 

Itaú, the State Department and our other partners are particularly excited about the role that we can play, as consumers and users of electronics, in managing what we buy and how we dispose of it. So let's start thinking about ways to activate our own networks to get involved – and use these insights to design concepts that leverage our collective people power.
