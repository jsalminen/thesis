Reflections: being an OpenIDEATOR

Anne-Laure Fayard

posted this on December 18, 2011 22:39

As part of my Exploring Creativity class, students had to register on the OpenIDEO platform as well as work in teams on the platform to develop a concept for the Unlawful detention challenge. We have a class blog "The Cafe" and as part of their class work, students have to put several reflections. The last one was called being an OpenIDEATOR.

Here is a short summary of the students' reflections which point to a positive and insightful experience (which I'm planning to offer again in my course in the spring):

- going beyond the fear of posting and being surprised by the positive and constructive feedback

- learning from the interactions and the feedback

- being surprised by the willingness of other OpenIDEATORS to help you improve your ideas

- learning that you can be creative in a collaborative manner and learning how to let go ownership's of ideas. This last point is not easy and also raised questions for some: who owned our ideas?

- OpenIDEO as a big brainstorming

- the importance of learning about the specific challenge and feeling that you might have an impact

- some individuals preferred one phase to another

- why can't we link, build upon concepts from other challenges? That will increase the pool of ideas and inspirations.

- Related to the class but that was applied for the challenge: the importance of prototyping to explore, articulate and develop ideas.
