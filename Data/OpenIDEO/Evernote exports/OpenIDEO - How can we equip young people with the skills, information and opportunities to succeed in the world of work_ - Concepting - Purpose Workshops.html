Purpose Workshops

Rather than answering the question "what do you want to be?", purpose workshops help young people to articulate what they want their life to be about.
 
http://www.backgroundsppt.net/uploads/person-with-question-mark-backgrounds-wallpapers.jpg
"What do you want to be when you grow up? A doctor? A lawyer? An engineer, perhaps? Possibly an architect? An accountant?". The holy quintet of of historically respectable professions, conversations with our young people continue in a similar form today. From a young age, we are encouraged to think of what job we want to do, rather than why we want to do it - a more fundamental question, surely. As the saying goes, the purpose of life is a life of purpose. I was very fortunate to be challenged a couple of years ago by a good friend when she asked me "What is your contribution going to be?". I wrote about the experience here and how it prompted me to articulate a purpose which, for me, frames a life worth living.


Purpose workshops are facilitated in-person and online brainstorming sessions where participants are supported to articulate their individual purpose - the contribution they wish to make to the world, their reason for being. While this will serve as a platform for exploring career options (i.e. how to give effect to their purpose), the focus is not on specific jobs.  Rather, the workshops will centre around:


- Values
- Aspirations
- What makes them feel good
- When they are old and looking back on their life, what will their life look like if it has been a success? 
- What do they want to have contributed to the world?


Together, these things will help develop something of a purpose statement which can be used to guide the individual's thinking about what careers might be suited to supporting that purpose. The idea is that this will lead to better career decisions and happier, more fulfilling lives.
 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

Arguably more so than previous generations, young people today are wanting more from life than a single-career and long-term job security. They want more of a work-life balance and will have a number of different careers. This points to a need for something more than an answer to "what job do you want?" and help with answering "what are you doing with your life?". Purpose workshops will help young people with the transition to the world of work by helping to answer that question so that they can see their jobs in the context of a broader purpose. As they look at different careers throughout their lives, they will have something against which to evaluate their opportunities and focus their thinking around education and training.
