Mint for e-waste management - What's my electronic footprint?

Empowering responsible e-waste management on an individual level through transparent reporting and clear assessment of discard options.
http://www.cisco.com/en/US/docs/switches/lan/energywise/phase2/orchestrator/administrator/guide/dashboard.html

This is a bit of a spinoff from "Gaming for E-Waste Behavior Change", based on the premise that giving users information about their actions and the effect of their actions can help them change their behavior.

What if there were a Mint.com style dashboard where users have a view to their entire electronic inventory, what that inventory translates into (each smartphone has LCD, lithium-ion battery, circuit board), and multiple options for managing/discarding their old electronics.

User scenario:
  -  I want to dispose of my electronics quickly and easily, what are the different options available to me? Are electronics really that harmful, should I just throw them out?

  -  A person inputs their current electronic devices into an online interface that has a dashboard to show the inventory of their electronics.

  -  The online site can then report metrics and data - what materials are in the device, what kind of environmental impact the device has, what it's worth

-  Whats my e-waste score or footprint?
-  Peer pressure - how does my ewaste footprint compare with neighbors?
-  Coopetition - can we get kids or schools to compete against each other for most sustainable?
-  How long until I achieve sustainability?
-  How are my purchase and discard choices affecting the environment?

  -  Options for reuse or recycle

-  Location of the nearest recycling center
-  The value of the device in the secondhand market
-  Perhaps a school or village that needs a donation of that specific device
-  Maybe a tax incentive for proper disposal with proof
-  Others?


How does your concept safeguard human health and protect our environment?


Transparency, clarity, accountability - The objective would be to allow users to gain insight into their e-waste, the effect it can have, and have a choice on how to manage their overall e-waste footprint.

Where does your concept fit into the lifecycle of electronic devices?


In the near term, this concept helps people find suitable options for disposing what they own. However, in the longer term it should influence purchase behavior by giving a deeper understanding of the impact of different products.



What steps could be taken today to start implementing your concept?


Variation on a concept - Perhaps there could be an opt-in, mint.com add-on. On the actual mint.com site, the add-on would collect info about electronics purchases and automatically upload the info to the site and instantly show the footprint of the components, value of resale, options for future reuse, etc.

Also, some solutions potentially exist for corporations. For example, http://www.ireuse.com/site/
