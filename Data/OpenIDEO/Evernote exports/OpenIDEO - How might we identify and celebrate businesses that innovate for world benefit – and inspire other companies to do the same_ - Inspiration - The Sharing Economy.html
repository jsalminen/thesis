The Sharing Economy

Everyone has a talent or skill they can "gift" to others. The world is full of unused, latent objects that can be valuable assets if placed in use or repurposed. How can we build a global exchange system to activate latent talent and objects?
Impossible: A New Gift Economy
The Sharing Economy is really an age-old idea which can be brought to global scale or drive local impact through the use of technology.  People worldwide have shared food, shelter, child care responsibilities, knowing it "takes a village".  While AirBNB http://bit.ly/VeqsTh and ZipCar http://www.zipcar.com/ have already disrupted the lodging and car ownership models, there are other examples to consider as well.

  • Imposssible:  A Gift Economy Network http://impossible.com/
  • Task Rabbit:  http://www.taskrabbit.com
  • NeighborGoods:  http://neighborgoods.net/
  • Freecycle:  http://www.freecycle.org/


Not only do these "exchanges" leverage latent assets, they create a spirit of abundance to those who may have seen scarcity. (I can afford a car, parking, insurance, etc. vs.  I can afford to share a care only when I need it; or Traveling and staying in hotels is lonely vs.  Sharing a room in someone's home not only gave me shelter, but a new experience). 

A Sharing Economy doesn't just address an economic need, it fills a human need to connect and feel valued as well.

How can we broaden the reach and depth of a Sharing Economy and imbed the philosophy into business interactions?

I love this new model! Here is another resource. A great article outlining sectors of the Sharing Economy / Collaborative Consumption: http://nextrends.swissnexsanfrancisco.org/forget-owning-lets-share/

Three Categories:

1. Product-service systems that facilitate the sharing or renting of a product, such as ZipCar or TechShop
2. Redistribution markets, which enable the re-ownership of a product, such as Craigslist or eBay
3. Collaborative lifestyles, in which assets and skills can be shared, such as CouchSurfing or TaskRabbit

Collaborative Consumption = (People x Stuff) + New Technologies = $$$
