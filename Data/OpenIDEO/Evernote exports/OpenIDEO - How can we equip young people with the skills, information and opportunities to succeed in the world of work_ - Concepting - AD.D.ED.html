AD.D.ED

AD.D.ED: Advertising. Design. Education. What if we combined advertising with some of the tools of design to continually educate young people about the information, skills and opportunities needed to succeed in the world of work?
Image: dailyinfographic.com

                                         AD.D.ED CAMPAIGN

                      "Master Your Major with a Minor Exploration"

Infographics pack a lot of useful data and information into graphically pleasing designs and can be used to creatively introduce young people to a variety of emerging industries and career opportunities.

These infographics would live both on and offline -- where one might typically find an Ad marketed at 16-24 year olds or the Millennial generation.

Using New York City as an example, infographic Ads can be displayed along a young person's daily commute -- on trains, near movie theaters, cafes, parks, music venues, schools, etc.

Artists who are influential in youth circles can be commissioned to paint murals of infographics where a young person might typically find a wall tagged with Graffitti or an Ad for a consumer product.

Companies who make products that young people utilize throughout their academic years can also be engaged in this awareness campaign.

For example, Mead and Marble use filler art on the covers of composition books and spiral notebooks.Instead, this handsome opportunity to connect with youth can be used to creatively introduce information about careers associated with a given subject  (I imagine using the covers as well as pages in between.  Think Moleskin and what they've done for writers and journals).

Brands: Picture an Apple iPhone surrounded by all of the people who work together and independently, to bring such a product to market.Each has a different title and a different professional background.

Bringing that amalgamation of people and ideas together in one Ad might be mind-boggling.But advertisers and designers can use their tools to bring this to life in a more creative campaign and for a variety of products that touch upon the daily lives of young people.

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

The residual effects of creatively equipping young people with vital information for 'composing their careers' (via a familiar but less traditional channel for such messaging), might set them on clearer and more engaged paths for reaching their professional goals.
