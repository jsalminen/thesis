My Story of Finding Inspiration & Direction in Life

Middle East- about to board the train to the airport for the next location!


I have my MBA & worked at the #1 brand company in the world leading global brand strategy and marketing on Olay. I had a great career & social life, but I felt like there was more to life.

 

I took a vacation to Africa to clear my head. While there I realized no amount of money was worth waking up &not feeling great. I didn’t know what the answer was, but I knew what it was not. It was not continuing to wake up unhappy. I ended up bungee jumping at Victoria Falls to prove I could do new &different things &be okay. I turned in my 2wk notice when I returned from vacation.

 

I started doing my own brand consulting to keep myself afloat financially.I felt like I needed to decompress though &sit w/my own thoughts before making any moves.

 

I went to a Buddhist temple to calm the chatter in my mind of “what’s next?” questions. Through meditation &living/working w/the community at the temple, I learned “self-love.”

 

Then my backpacking begun! I left on a 1-way ticket &explored 50 countries in 8 months. While away from my normal environment, I reflected on what made me sad/angry/happy &set goals for what I wanted to do with my life. I had gotten sucked into being a fast-mover &didn’t give myself time to process how I was living. The experience of leaving my “known” job for the unknown taught me that it’s okay to love yourself &put your own needs above others at times if it helps you be a more whole, balanced, individual.

 

My story is simple, but the lessons learned can make a significant difference in leading a fulfilling life:

1)      Remove external noise. We need time inside our own minds to hone our gut instincts.

2)      Do something that scares you. Pushing yourself outside your comfort zone will help you learn more about yourself than in a classroom, reading books, or talking to friends/ family.

3)      Follow a dream to build confidence in yourself. Pick one dream you have and go for it! It doesn’t matter how “big” or “small” the endeavor may be. Don’t let life pass you by living with potential.
