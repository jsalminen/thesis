Applause vs Evaluation

Paul Reader

posted this on May 29 03:54

Applause



In a traditional sense applause is showing appreciation - so you can applaud an inspiration or concept at any time.



During the Applause Phase you are encouraged to "applaud" the Concepts that you consider "praiseworthy" and this helps the OpenIDEO Team to gauge community opinion on the whole range of concepts (in the Vibrancy challenge there were 329 initial concepts to choose between). Because your applause for concepts is used in this way you can only applaud a concept once.

Of course applause is not the sole determinant in choosing the shortlist of concepts for refinement and evaluation, and applauding after the applause phase will not be taken into account for shortlist purposes but it does indicate to the originator(s) of the concept that you appreciate their efforts.



However, I hope you have noticed that Inspirations and Concepts are not the only things that can be applauded.

You can applaud comments too. This adds a touch of appreciation for the content of comments which might be simple encouragement, new perspectives on ideas in the concept or even new directions for the concept to grow - in short "collaboration". Applauding comments lets the person making them know that others support them. Perhaps its only a nice (anonymous) touch of appreciation but it helps build encouragement throughout the community.



Have a little think about the Applause you give - if OpenIDEO had wanted to make it simply a popularity poll they would probably have used a "like" button (a la Facebook et al) - as I said at the start applause is appreciation not popularity and I (personally) believe the distinction is important.



Evaluation



While applause is about appreciation, evaluation is about critically examining the shortlisted concepts in the context of the challenge. That is why you will find a series of guided questions and multiple choice answers dominates the evaluation (even if on some challenges your open comments are sought too). Before undertaking your first evaluation it is definitely a good idea to view the Evaluation Video (as Arjan Tupan suggests inÂ How To... Evaluate concepts in the Evaluation Phase ).

I should like to endorse the advice given in that video and add two further comments.

First, as with applause, evaluation is not a popularity poll although your evaluation again contributes to the challenge teams deliberations. In this context I would still encourage you to get friends and family "on board" and encourage them to participate, not only in supporting your concept through evaluation, but in the whole challenge process.

Secondly, for everyone participating in the evaluation phase I would encourage you to objectively evaluate ALL the shortlisted concepts. In my own evaluations I find it helpful to think first about how a particular concept fits the needs of the whole challenge and then also how concepts fit together as a suite of solutions to the original problem.
