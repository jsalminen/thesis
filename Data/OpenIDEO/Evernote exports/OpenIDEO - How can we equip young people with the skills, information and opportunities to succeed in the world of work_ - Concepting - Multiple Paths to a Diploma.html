Multiple Paths to a Diploma

Everyone is different, and so should their paths towards their diploma. Far too often students are stifled by the rigidity of curriculum. A model that encourages students to seek education in all aspects of life doesn't just enlighten, but empowers.
Gardening for a health credit.

Introduction

When people are working towards a degree, they are often doing so by a prescribed curriculum that sets the path of their education in stone. This was developed because educators and politicians wanted a way to benchmark student progress before delivering a degree. However, with information being freely available in the world, and ideas and innovation causing rapid changes in all industries, is it still the correct model to follow?

For some, the guidance is desired and needed. However, I argue that if others are motivated to seek education and development, there might be a better way. For them, I believe a platform that allows students of all walks to track their progress and development, still reaching benchmarks that match with curriculum that focuses on what we want students to get out of their exploration, and not just time spent in a seat.

The Curriculum

Curriculum is meant to ensure people are leaving an institution having met expectations to receive a degree. In its own right, that is not a bad idea. However, the focus needs to be on the individual and their personal path of learning, not how many hours a student has spent sitting at a desk listening to a lecture.

To accomplish this, educators sit down and spent significant time on the question, "What do our students need to succeed, and why?" Do they need to have read a certain number of books? Ability to research and construct ideas for argument? Ability to solve Calculus? A love of learning? The idea of requirement needs to be reconsidered, and not believed that since it was good enough for one generation that it is fit for the next.

The Avenues for Education

Though certain requirements would still need to be recognized and fulfilled, the means of doing so could be endless:

  • Health Requirement: participation in a community garden
  • History Requirement: working in the city archives to gather pertinent information for an upcoming piece of legislation
  • Science Requirement: view videos on EdX
  • English Requirement: apprentice at a local small-run publisher
  • Social Studies Requirement: volunteer at a local community center
  • Art Requirement: beautify the neighborhood
  • Physical Education Requirement: ride a bike to school for a semester

These are just a few examples, but it is easy to see how there are many opportunities to be turned into an educational event. Students are still well-rounded and experienced, but in ways that are more meaningful to them.

Conclusion

Allowing students multiple ways to graduation encourages them to take control of their own education, and view their life as a series of educational opportunities. They will see life in a whole new way, looking for the lesson and take-away in each lesson. The education will not stop once they leave the building, but continue as they weave their way through life, adding to their ever-growing transcript.

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

By encouraging students to take control of their education, and see opportunities for learning everywhere, they will be confident to step out of their comfort zone as they look for work, and willing to start some place they did not expect. The lessons they take from each experience will be beneficial for their entire lives, and recognizing that possibility encourages people to seek out such events, and not sit back and wait for opportunities to simply present themselves.

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

Guiding students to take control of their education will encourage them to take control of their own life, seek out opportunities, and make the most of every experience. This will create well-rounded, highly motivated individuals that will be successful in the workplace.
