Reverse Mentorship

An idea that Jack Welch made popular long ago seems to be back on the rise — getting someone 5, 10, 15 or even 20 years your junior to help you see the world from an entirely different perspective. And in return, you can do the same for them!
 
Reverse Mentorship.
 
We so often consider a mentorship to be only about the "old teaching the young," but I can say for sure that I've learned more about up-and-coming technologies from some of my nieces than many of my professors. Reverse mentorships give well-seasoned career vets insight into an age demographic they might otherwise be entirely out of touch with — and in return, you can teach them everything you wish you'd known when you were 20.
