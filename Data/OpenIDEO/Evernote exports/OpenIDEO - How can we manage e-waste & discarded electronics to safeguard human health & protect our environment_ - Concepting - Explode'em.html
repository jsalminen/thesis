Explode'em

Drawing an exploded view might sound familiar to people with an engineering or design background. How about taking apart discarded electronics and building a library of real exploded items?
 
 
 
In a design or engineering education, drawing an exploded view is one of the most fun tasks we are challenged with. The case is usually that we are asked to bring a broken device from home, take it apart and draw it. When we are done, what do we do with this exploded item?



Even today, whenever I catch someone throw away an electronic device, I stop them and take this thing simply to explode and see what there is inside. This curiosity has now turned into the idea of having a library of real exploded items.





 
 

How does your concept safeguard human health and protect our environment?

Explode'em does not directly try to safeguard human health or protect the environment. It suggests a fun way to make use of e-waste and discarded electronics in education.
 

Where does your concept fit into the lifecycle of electronic devices?

By taking apart electronics and exhibiting them, technology is no longer hidden inside ergonomic plastic shells. In a way, it shows us drastically everything we throw away when we put an electric device in the garbage bin.
 

What steps could be taken today to start implementing your concept?

Today, any visualization class tutor can ask students to exhibit the items they took apart, in addition to their exploded view drawings. Further on, universities can be collaborated to provide space and human resources, as exploded views of items is within their interest already.
