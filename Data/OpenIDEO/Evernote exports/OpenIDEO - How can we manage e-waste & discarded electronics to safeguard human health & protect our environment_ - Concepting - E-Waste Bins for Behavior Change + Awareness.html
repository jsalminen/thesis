E-Waste Bins for Behavior Change + Awareness

At work we have three bins for compost, recycling and landfill. What if there were a fourth bin for e-waste?

Every time I use the kitchen in the office, I'm prompted to consider what kind of waste I'm producing and how to sort it. Is it compostable? Can it be recycling? Or is it just trash? What if there were a fourth bin that was labeled E-Waste?

Moreover, what if the signage (as you can see here, there are signs that tell us what's compost, for instance) actually listed bigger e-waste options like computers, printers, computer batteries, hair dryers etc? Clearly you wouldn't leave a computer in a tiny bin – but might it provoke people to see their electronic gadgets in a new way if they start seeing them as things that need to go 'somewhere' when they're no longer in use?

How does your concept safeguard human health and protect our environment?

– it encourages people to recycle/sort their e-waste (like all those batteries you collect)
– it offers a subtle, gentle reminder that e-waste is another type of waste
– it may also prompt people to think more broadly about their electronics 'habits' –> almost like an art installation that nudges you to think about the issues!

Where does your concept fit into the lifecycle of electronic devices?

Helps people understand that e-waste is actually waste and needs to be managed properly.

What steps could be taken today to start implementing your concept?

We could try prototyping this in our office and you could too! :)

What kinds of resources will be needed to fully implement and scale your concept?

Ultimately you want these bins to be used – so we'd have to make sure that our office had the right vendor connections to ensure safe pick up and processing of the e-waste. It may cost money to forge a partnership with an e-waste broker who could take the discarded items away.

It seems as though there's a great opportunity for collaboration with DFA's concept re: the safety of e-waste collection units: http://www.openideo.com/open/e-waste/concepting/safety-is-important-too/. While one goal of yours might be to have all of the units look similar, there's likely a need for adding some additional safeguards to the e-waste bin.
