Video Gaming to Save the Generation

10,000 hours. That's the amount of hours you need to invest become excellent at something. When I heard this number I was really scared but then I looked at this statistic and I realized that people all around me are already putting in these hours.
 
 
 
Almost 3 billion hours are currently being spent on gaming Every WEEK. By the time the average American turns 21 they have spent more than 10,000 hours playing video games. That is equivalent to 5 years of work at a full time job.

 

A video game that is actually designed in a way that the user feels like they are playing a game but they are actually picking up some very important skills on the way. They start this game at an initial level and lets say they pick art as something they want to study. You have to keep practicing and posting about it online and the online community helps you by giving you feedback.

 

You move on to the next stage if you keep the feedbacks in mind and make the changes to your project according.

 

The hours the user is putting in to their projects are posted online on their profile.

 

When you reach the final stage of the game the community is asked to vote on one person that has made the best progress in the 10,000 hours.

 

If you keep doing this for your respective field over and over again you will be one of the best in your field. You will have a supportive community that already knows about you.

 

This concept is also good because the job you end up with is something that you are very good at and love a lot since you have spent so much time polishing your skills.



 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

I hope that this concept can be used by young people to understand what they are good at and practice until they become excellent at it. This will also equip the users with networks that are very helpful.
 

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

All the components will be online. But the most important component will be the user posting their work, getting feedback and building upon those feedbacks.
 

My Virtual Team

Nickolai Sampson

Steadily, gaming concepts (piecemeal rewards, viewable progress, achievement badges, etc.) are being incorporated into learning systems (take a look at what Khan Academy does). As for the 10,000 hours "rule", that is a very generalized number taken from the field of skill acquisition and expertise. take a look at this article: http://www.openeducation.net/2008/11/02/deliberate-practice-%E2%80%93-where-self-reflection-work-ethic-and-ambition-meet/ for a more thorough explanation of deliberate practice than what Malcolm Gladwell offers. Any attempt to equip people with skills should take advantage of research over the last 20 years into skill acquisition and expertise.



A couple other texts that you might find helpful/interesting are Talent is Overrated by Geoff Colvin and Bounce by Matthew Syed.
