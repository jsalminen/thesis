Meet my Laptop- her name is Emma Slate

When I started college I had a friend who would give weird names to everything. She named my ring Theodore Snowflake, her scarf was Antoinette Marie III. The closer we got, the more we started fabricating each others personality traits.

My first laptop was IBM t60, and even though it was quite heavy it was my best friend, after five years of being together it passed away, we went through two hard drive replacements, 2 different OS updates, and a bunch of keys breaking.

Finally when I couldn't spend anymore more money on its upkeep, I though it would be a good idea to buy a new laptop and we (me and my friends name her Emma Slate).  Anytime anyone wants to borrow my laptop now they always say "Can I have Emma?"


I feel like naming my devices has made me appreciate them, their history, their background.  Last week when Emma stopped working I felt really horrible and I worked on it for a few days until she was up and running again.  She is up and running again and faster than ever. 

What I am saying is maybe we should be more loyal to our choices. 


How does your concept safeguard human health and protect our environment?

Rather than buying a smart phone every six months or every year, people can probably buy one phone every 2-3 years maybe.

Where does your concept fit into the lifecycle of electronic devices?

This concept can be used to change the consumers purchase behavior.

What steps could be taken today to start implementing your concept?

It can be different for everyone. In the sea of technology, pick the best choice for you and customize it to your needs.
