Give a day!

Every year schools donate one day, where the students go out and work. They get to pick a business, and ask for a job for the "give a day program" The business gives a sett amount of money, which is given to a charity.
 
From www.od.no which is a Norwegian give a day - program.
Students for a sett age, say 10 years and up. Have a day a year, where they work for a business of their choice. The business gives a small amount of money, ex 20 $, but can give more. The money goes to a charity. The students learns about the charity and cause, what the money goes to, and what impact it will do. 


The schools can set of one week for educational purposes, where the students learn about different subjects around global issues, entrepreneuring, job applications, networking and so on. 
By starting at an early age, every year the subjects can go more and more over towards preparing  the students for work marked, and the adult life. 



Give a day gives the students work experience, and the opportunity to work in different types of industries. They will get to know people in the business, which can make it easier to get jobs later on. 


The business does something nice, without loosing money. 


Inspired by http://www.odwusa.org/  Operation Day's Work,  but it could build more towards work life, even though the money will be given to a charity. 
 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

The work experience, and the contacts they make threw the years will make it a lot easier to get work later on. They will also be able to try out different types of jobs, so maybe they get an impression of what they wish to do.
 

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

This would be done as a nonprofit organization, that controls the charity of the year, and the fallow ups. They collect the money and sends one big pot to the cause, and sends feedback to the schools of what the money has been used for. They can also put up a schedule our week curriculum, what students should learn that week.



With the right PR, and by contacting as many schools as possible, the word will be spread. Hopefully the schools will see what a great chance and opportunity this is for the kids, and join. After all, their job is to prepare them for the real wold!
