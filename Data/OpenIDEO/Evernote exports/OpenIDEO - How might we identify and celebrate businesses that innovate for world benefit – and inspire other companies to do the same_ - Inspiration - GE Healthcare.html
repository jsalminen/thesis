GE Healthcare

Innovative new technology allows doctors to carry sophisticated technology to remote areas.

GE leveraged innovation for an emerging market by taking a sophisticated piece of equipment (an ECG machine) and adapting it for the Indian market. The machine was designed more specifically for travelling doctors who go to remote areas where the infrastructure and economy is not as well developed. The machine was made light and “dust-proof” to increase it portability and endurance, making it possible to bring an ECG machine on a rural bus. The affordability of the machine also makes the machine much more accessible to his patients, as doctors will now be able to charge much lower rates to their patients.

This sort of innovation is a great example of how companies are forced to be innovative to create a product that is aimed at less developed areas of the world. Large companies like GE, in order to stay competitive in rural India need to be very adaptive and innovative to create high-tech products that are relevant to economies that previously were not able to use this sort of product. It is very interesting to note that being able to reach less developed areas requires cutting-edge technology and a great deal of engineering.
