Career-Coffee Mobile Truck {Mission in a Box}

Bringing career advice to young people in an easy, fun and approachable way. Following the concept of a Mobile Medical Unit that brings primary care to under-served areas the Mobile Truck will provide primary counseling with a fun twist.
 
Mission on the move!
 

The Career-Coffee Mobile Truck will make career advice accessible to young people in a convenient way. By making coaching reachable and approachable in a fun public environment will empower young people’s confidence. Following the ING Direct Café Concept, the CC Mobile Truck is a place where young people can be heard and understood. Where they can exhale, regroup and gain perspective to a better career path. (http://www.ingdirect.ca/en/aboutus/contactus/cafes/index.html)

The Career-Coffee Mobile Truck is a fun place that appeals to a young generation. Muvbox concept of a sustainable, solar, transformable container can be an inspiration for the design of the truck. (http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GYywrXOyGak&feature=plcp)

Sample Scenario

Daniel is 23 years old and graduated with a degree in finance 1 year ago, since then he has not been able to find a job in his career path. In order for him to survive and get some experience he has been helping his uncle who owns a grocery store.

He finally got a job interview in his field of study that he’s is very excited about. Since he does not have experience in his field of study he knows he needs interview practice, skills refreshing, and preparation.

He heard through his friends about a Mobile Truck offering career advice, whose location is announced through Twitter.

The Mobile Truck travels around the city providing free counseling, mock interviews, career planning, strengths assessments, career change guidance, resume reviews, online marketing support (LinkedIn, twitter, etc) and best of all great coffee!!!

The next morning Daniel heads over and meets with Joe, the counselor on board. He is nervous at first but after a few minutes Joe makes him feel at home, in a friendly environment in front of a hot cup of coffee. Daniel leaves with a personalized plan to prepare for the interview and is scheduled for a skills refresher course and for a mock interview in a couple of days.

After his course and his mock interview, he goes with confidence to his real interview.

A week has passed since Daniel got the job and he receives a phone call from Joe to ask him how he is doing. He could not be happier!

One year has passed and Daniel loves his job. During the past year he has been going to the Mobile Truck on a regular basis. He loves the coffee and he reviews his career goals. He recommends the Mobile Truck to all his friends.

 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

Social Media plays an important part of the concept to engage the community.

Twitter, Foursquare Check-ins, Facebook Groups, MeetUps and a promotional website for meeting online bookings and forum discussions.
