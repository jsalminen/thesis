Organic Tech - like Organic Food

Simple "Organic" label appears prominently on certified organic tech products, as a growing eHealth-conscious community demands organic - shifting the market to a better place. The Organic Food movement is an inspiration.
 
Label based on the USDA certified organic tag for food
The simple visual label creates actionable information (its either organic or not) for consumers, a means to differentiate for early-adopter producers and finally the business case for a cleaner ecosystem.




1. A simple universally understood visual label guides user behavior
It becomes a simple proxy for a complex determination, just like an Organic Food tag. The Organic label is non-technical and the information conveyed can be immediately acted upon. This is fundamentally different from some current efforts such as epeat (http://epeat.net) which have three different classifications for electronics and further scores that are not even displayed on product or packaging. Here, a tech product either is certified Organic or not. Just like the milk. 


2. User behavior influences producer behavior
A community of early enthusiasts grows into a mass movement demanding certified Organic Tech products, eventually changing the game by having a significant impact on how companies produce and manage the lifecycle of their goods.


3. 1 & 2 result in healthy ecosystem 
An info-graphic about the impact due to green gadgets can be seen here: http://www.epeat.net/2012/03/news/earth-911-infographic-ecobenefits-of-green-gadgets/. The impact due to Organic Tech can be understood in similar terms but will be much greater in scale.





The definition of "Organic Tech" will include multiple factors such as recyclability, upgradability, longevity etc. The definition will be written in a way that encourages desirable human and environmental results E.g. any non-recoverable lead in a product could make it not organic. On the other hand, specifying how much lead it contains and how that is to be recovered, can (together with other criteria) earn a product the very desirable "Organic" tag.



Challenges
1. Bootstrapping
2. The self-interest incentive in choosing Organic is not as obvious for technology products as it is for food
 
 

How does your concept safeguard human health and protect our environment?

Human health and the the environment benefit because:

1. Consumers choose better products which are now easily and visually identified.

2. Producers create better products in order to earn the valued Organic tag

3. The ecosystem shifts to a better place with sustainable practices
 

Where does your concept fit into the lifecycle of electronic devices?

The Organic Tech movement

1. Encourages consumers to change their buying behavior by preferring and demanding "Organic Tech" products.

2. Improves consumer awareness thru a proliferation of the visual label and related conversations

3. Encourages companies to design and create better products to initially differentiate and eventually meet an expected standard

 

What steps could be taken today to start implementing your concept?

0. Identify the owner/driver for the initiative

1. Work with epeat (or similar organization) to agree on the first cut definition and certification mechanism of an "Organic Tech" product

2. Identify companies willing to be the first to carry the Organic label on their product(s)

3. Create an engaged consumer community to follow the progress, discuss refinements. pre-order cool organic products etc.

4. Get the conversation going on social media, in the press/blogosphere, thru internet memes and celebrity endorsement
 

What kinds of resources will be needed to fully implement and scale your concept?

We will need

1. An engaged and growing community that will buy and demand Organic tech products, and encourage their friends and organizations to do the same

2. Partnership with organizations and/or governments to maintain definition and mechanisms of certification.

3. Partnership with companies keen on carrying the Organic Tech label, at least until the market dynamics take over.

4. Money to run the driving body, to engage in marketing and biz dev etc until the ecosystem evolves to be self sustaining.



The Organic Food movement can be studied for inspiration.
