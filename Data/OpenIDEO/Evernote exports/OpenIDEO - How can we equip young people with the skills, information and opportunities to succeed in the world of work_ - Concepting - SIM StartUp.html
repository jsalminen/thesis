SIM StartUp

Modular gaming platform to enable someone at any age or any skill level to learn the techniques necessary to design a product... a web service... a building design... from the ground up.
 
Hypothetical game scenario

This is an engaging and collaborative “Legos”-like approach to unlocking technical skills. The “epic win” would link the player with a mentor to frame the result in a manner that is presentable in a professional scenario, turning your gaming wins into a portfolio of technical work.



The game can be set up in a Sim City or World of Warcraft-like graphical scenario. A player starts with a specific game platform that caters to a general area of interest, such as the following examples:

 

  • Product design
  • Automotive design
  • Architecture
  • Veterinary care
  • Graphics design
  • Web service

 

LEGO-INSPIRED MODULES

Initial modules are given that act as “Lego” blocks. The player tackles each block to crack it. Each unlocked block provides a new ability, component, or collaborator that can be used towards the ultimate effort.



 

For example, in the automotive design game scenario, an unlocked block could be a servo motor that can be linked to other blocks (e.g., wheels, axles, batteries) to generate an electric car. In the process, the player must solve puzzles or game scenarios that provide the skills needed to work with each component (block). The player must also learn how blocks are inter-related, such as which blocks can be linked to provide a more useful tool (thus, the Legos analogy).



(maybe this isn't the best way... how else could we capture technical skills within modules that can be linked together and built upon?)

 

ONLINE COLLABORATION

Some blocks may only be unlockable through collaborative effort, which could be achieved through online game interaction with other players. For example, the automotive design player needs to link with a race track design player to test their car.

 

"EPIC WINS"

Gaming levels may exist that provide distinct “wins” for the player, unlocking new block packages and collaboration abilities. At advanced levels, mentor blocks may be unlocked to link gamers with experts in a field. A portfolio could be developed at this level based on the final outcome. This could be done at a level that may be presentable in an application or interview, or it could provide an IDEO achievement award that gives young players confidence in their abilities to tackle big and new challenges. Perhaps there could be a small conference dedicated to the innovative designs achieved in this gaming world.



(what else would provide the motivation to play this game long-term and provide the skills young people need to crack into the job market?)
 
 

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?



• Teach design flow from beginning to end

• Teach the "language" of a field

• Encourage online collaboration to achieve game endpoints

• Provide a professional portfolio to show the work achieved

• Provide a mentoring contact in the field
 

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?



• Committed gamers will achieve a game level that provides a mentor and portfolio development based on the game endpoint

• Unlocking "blocks" in the game provide small steps towards learning a larger design process within a field

• Working with other players brings the online gaming component to a realistic teamwork level
