Startup Pirates

Startup Pirates is a one-week acceleration program for young entrepreneurs in the making.
Image explain the concept of Startup Pirates.

Startup Pirates is a one-week acceleration program for young entrepreneurs in the making. Being an entrepreneur is not only about creating a new company/start-up is much more about taking the responsibility for our own life and define a path to follow. With this in mind, we created Startup Pirates where participants develop a business idea while learning lots of valuable lessons for the future professional lives whether or not they decide to become entrepreneurs.

In a very intensive week, Startup Pirates combines training, mentoring and idea development and helps young people to structure and develop their business ideas. In the end, they have the opportunity to pitch their ideas to investors and successful business leaders.

Startup Pirates in numbers

- 8 days

- +30 Participants

- +30 guests (including mentors, investors and entrepreneurs)

- 8 workshops

- 14 working sessions

- 2 sessions “Ask the Mentor”

- 2 entrepreneur talks

- 2 other activities

How will your concept support young people as they transition into the world of work?

We believe that Startup Pirates will provide young people the tools, the network and the information they need to kick start their professional career in a more confident way. In a week, they will work in teams, meet more than 30 entrepreneurs and investors, develop a business idea and study very practical and useful concepts that will help then in their professional lives.
A good transition into the world of work has a lot to do with being confident about our own skills, know what we are good and not that good at and be clear in define what do we have as goals. After that the all process is much easier.

What online or in-person components of your concept will best support this transition?

The components of this concept will be primarily offline to make sure that every individual has customize attention and a program design to their needs. However, we also plan to have a online platform where we can share the videos of the talks and workshops so we can easily scale and reach a wider audience.
