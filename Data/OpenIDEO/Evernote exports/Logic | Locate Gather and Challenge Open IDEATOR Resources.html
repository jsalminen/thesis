Open IDEATOR Resources

This site is a prototype inspired by some of the concepts generated in last year’s
‘Social Impact Challenge’. It is limited in scope, being hosted on a ‘free’ hosting site, but hopefully may be sufficient for ideators to try out some ‘working’ aspects of the concepts. Built with off-the-shelf components it cannot look exactly like the concepts, but illustrates some possibilities.
A number of OpenIDEATORs have recently requested a means of collaborating on the creation of Inspirations and Concepts, and in particular the possibility of combining Concepts. Obviously this site cannot ‘interfere’ with the structure and functionality of the OpenIDEO site itself but I have updated it to use BP Docs, incorporate a simple spreadsheet page (if requested), add Virtual Teams in the groups area, allow a limited doodle (drawing) capacity. All of which I hope illustrates the potential for such a collaborative environment on the OpenIDEO site itself.

If you are not a member of OpenIDEO please visit that site first (click on the image above). We hope you will like what you see and choose to participate.

Site Features

Tools to support OpenIDEATOR Collaboration both on and off this site.
(summarised and detailed below)

In brief:

  • Locate – IDEATOR Directory and Geolocation incorporating member, location and talent (skills) searching
  • Gather – Events Management (incl Bookings) & Venue Geolocation
  • Challenge – IDEATOR Endorsed Nascent Challenge Listings
  • Teams – IDEATOR Local Chapter and Concept Virtual Teams Management incorporating both events and shared documents.
  • Dictionaries – descriptions and links to external resources.
  • General – further site information and operating constraints.

And in more detail (use the links from the above list):

Locate
(return to features list)

This is the IDEATOR (Members) Directory page with a world map for visual location of other ideators. (Not the map in the sidebar, that is visitor statistics.)

Both the ideator list and the map have links to ideator public profile pages where individual location maps are included.

Although adding ‘location’ is optional in the sign-up form you are encouraged to include it, but restrict information to city, state, and country only. If you wish to share greater detail you can do so, privately, on an individual basis, either on this site or elsewhere.

Searching for Members

You can search for members by name, location and talent.

Several suggestions were made in the Social Impact challenge for an ideator talent/skill pool or list – the site has a limited application of this.
On the member registration page (or by editing one’s own public profile) members can provide a list of talents (e.g.psychologist, web designer, graphic artist) using commas to separate list items.

On the Locate page there are two ways to search for members: the default Member Search Box can be used to search for individual words or part words on members profiles.

Additionally the Profile Search Form can be used to search the extended profile fields concurrently so searching for ‘Melbourne’ in Location and ‘graphic designer’ in Talents will list all members who are graphic designers and live in Melbourne.

Gather
(return to features list)

This uses an established Event Manager component that provides most essential requirements including GCal and ICal feeds.

Events are ideator driven (but see temporary moderation arrangements below).
The time zone has been set to American Samoa. Users adding events using their local dates and times will not see them expire until the date changes in Pago Pago – thus gatherings set up for western USA will still be visible to others around the world. Discussion’s continue with the developer to understand how the date/time shown on the event reflects the timezone of the author and the visitor.
(If you are happy to try this out we can probably discover the practicalities of it together).

It’s not as flashy as meetup.com but not as crowded either – and could be dedicated to OpenIDEO requirements.

Registered ideators can submit events for listing and designated members can approve them.. This means Local Chapter administrators, secretaries etc. or even individuals can set up a gathering, and while listing is moderated it does not require separate ‘organisations’ or ‘meet-up sites’ with dedicated administrators.
Gatherings can be categorised (Tweetup, OpenStorm, Local Chapter Meeting, Collaboratory) – with additional OpenIDEO specific descriptions as required.

Gathering organisers can choose to add bookings as appropriate (in at least one example I have included a ticket category of Virtual – that would allow for things like video conferencing) – it’s basically quite flexible.

Challenge
(return to features list)

IDEATOR initiated and endorsed challenges with varying scope were a feature of a number of concepts. The blog component of this site has been dedicated to providing a list of nascent challenges.
Blog titles are used for the challenge statement and blog content for the brief. Actual discussion or development on a challenge would occur elsewhere (like OpenIDEO or some other site dedicated to the purpose).
The scope of the challenge (Local, Regional, National, Open) can be indicated by choosing an appropriate category at the time of creation.
Three other categories/sub-categories have been supplied (Adopted, -Sponsored, -Realised) which can be used to indicate broad action being taken on a listed challenge.

A simple voting system has been incorporated for possible IDEATOR endorsement.
Tags are available to assist in providing key search terms for challenges.

Teams
(return to features list)

This is the groups component of Buddypress rebadged.

Any ideator can request the capacity to create a team. At present this is moderated following a tendency for some non-ideators to create inappropriate groups.

Teams fall into two broad categories public and private:

  • Public teams will be such groups as OpenIDEO Local Chapters (e.g. Perth Local Chapter) or Collaboratories.
  • Private teams might be characterised by OpenIDEO concept Virtual Teams.

It is possible to create team gatherings, both single and recurrent.

Dictionaries
(return to features list)

These don’t originate in the Social Impact challenge. Nevertheless this page has brief descriptions for the newly established Founder’s Dictionary (an entrepreneurial tool) and a proposal for a companion Designer’s Dictionary to assist ideators.

General
(return to features list)

As indicated this is a prototype/test site and has limited data capacity. One of the constraints of the hosting is email traffic so I am hoping that my limits will not be exceeded. Almost all aspects are moderated at the moment, mainly to avoid unsavoury/frivolous use of the site. If you are concerned about personal information you are welcome to use fictitious information – however, including your OpenIDEO name in Personal Details will assist me in identifying real ideators (that name is not made public on this site).

I will include a few fictitious events and challenges to kick-start it then perhaps add a few more from time to time if people are using the site.

I have tried to anticipate some questions on the FAQ page and if you need guidance for using the site please visit the various How_To pages.

Anyone wanting greater access, to make suggestions, ask questions or collaborate can contact me through Open IDEO or through PM on this site. I hope there may be someone in the OpenIDEO community with a greater knowledge of WordPress/Buddypress than me.

Wherever possible I have tried to link back to OpenIDEO so that casual visitors to this site can gain some exposure to the OpenIDEO Community. I hope the OpenIDEO Team will forgive me for taking a small snapshot of the logo to use as a link graphic.

I have deliberately chosen a hosting provider without advertising so that the only links included will either be internal, links to OpenIDEO, links to the plugin providers or links to ‘challenge’ pages (these will be vetted before blog posts or edits are approved). Hopefully I can keep the site ‘clean’ while IDEATORS take a look, and I will consider restricting site membership if necessary.
